**Market Overview and Growth Potential:**

* India's autonomous vehicle market is worth ₹21,000 crores today and is expected to reach ₹1.9 lakh crores by 2030.
* 24.3% annual growth rate.
* Market size jumped from ₹17,000 crores in 2023 to ₹21,000 crores in 2024.

**Key Market Segments:**

* Commercial vehicles (trucking solutions in ports and factories).
* Logistics (reducing driver costs and improving safety).
* Two-wheeler ADAS (safety systems for motorcycles and scooters).
* Industrial applications (warehouses, mines, and construction sites).

**Government Initiatives and Policy Support:**

* Motor Vehicles Amendment Act 2019: allows testing of autonomous vehicles on Indian roads.
* FAME-II Scheme Integration: allocates ₹10,000 crores over five years to promote electric vehicle adoption, including support for autonomous electric vehicles.
* Bharat NCAP and Safety Standards: evaluating ADAS (Advanced Driver Assistance Systems) capabilities, making safety tech mandatory for higher ratings.
* Smart Cities Mission Support: allocating approximately $1.5 billion to develop 100 smart cities, with 60 already participating in intelligent transportation initiatives.
* R&D and Innovation Support: government provides R&D subsidies and reduced import duties on autonomous vehicle components.

**Leading Indian Autonomous Vehicle Startups:**

* Swaayatt Robots: founded in 2015, location Bhopal, MP, funding $4 million, achieved Level 5 autonomous driving system.
* Minus Zero: founded in 2021, location Bengaluru, Karnataka, funding $1.8 million, uses "Nature-inspired AI" technology.

**Technical Details:**

* Swaayatt Robots' mathematical and probabilistic approach reduces dependence on expensive sensors like LiDAR, using sparse maps and AI algorithms to handle unpredictable Indian traffic patterns.
* Minus Zero's "Nature-inspired AI" technology learns from massive amounts of unstructured driving data, uses foundational AI models, and works with just cameras (no expensive LiDAR).
