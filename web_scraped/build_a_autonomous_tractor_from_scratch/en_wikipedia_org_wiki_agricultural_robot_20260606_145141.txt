**Applications:**

* Harvesting stage
* Weed control
* Planting seeds
* Harvesting
* Environmental monitoring
* Soil analysis

**Designs:**

* End effector: device found at the end of the robotic arm, used for various agricultural operations
* Manipulator: allows the gripper and end effector to navigate through their environment
* Gripper: grasping device used for harvesting the target crop
* End effector designs:
	+ Harvesting: designed to grasp, cut, and push bunches of grapes
	+ Berry thinning: designed to cut off rachis branches and extract the bunch of grapes
	+ Spraying: designed to distribute chemical liquid evenly across the bunch
	+ Bagging: designed to contain the grapes in bunches
* Manipulator design:
	+ Four-bar parallel links that maintain the gripper's position and height
	+ Pneumatic actuators: motors which produce linear and rotary motion by converting compressed air into energy
* Gripper design:
	+ Two mechanical fingers that can move in synchrony
	+ Equipped with a sharp blade in the procedure that required plants to be cut for harvesting

**Development:**

* Early development of robotics in agriculture dated back to the 1920s
* Research on automatic vehicle guidance began in the 1920s and 1950s
* Autonomous agricultural vehicles developed in the 1960s
* Development of robots in agriculture continued to advance as technologies in other sectors developed
* Concept of autonomous agricultural vehicles was not perfect, still needing a cable system to guide their path, until the 1980s following the development of the computer.
