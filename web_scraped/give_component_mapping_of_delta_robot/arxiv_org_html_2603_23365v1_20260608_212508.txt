**PinPoint Framework:**

* Probabilistic variational inference framework for real-time monocular needle pose estimation
* Formulates needle tracking as inference over SE(3)
* Maintains a distribution of pose hypotheses, preserving the genuine multimodal structure of the posterior
* Geometric constraints from monocular image observations and physical robot-grasp are expressed as analytical residuals with closed-form Jacobians
* Stein Variational Newton (SVN) inference with Gauss-Newton curvature for efficient convergence and hypothesis diversity preservation

**Evaluation Results:**

* Mean translational error reduced by 80% (down to 1.00 mm) and rotational error by 78% (down to 13.80°) relative to particle-filter baseline
* On induced-rotation sequences, PinPoint maintains a bimodal posterior 84% of the time, compared to 28% for particle filter baseline
* Average errors during occlusion: 1.34 mm in translation and 19.18° in rotation

**Monocular Needle Pose Estimation:**

* Inherently ill-posed problem due to depth ambiguity and rotational symmetry
* Multiple 3D configurations can produce similar image projections
* Probabilistic filters can recover a single reliable pose estimate in stereoscopic settings, but not in monocular settings

**Robotic Suturing:**

* One of the most fundamental and cognitively demanding tasks in Robot-assisted Minimally Invasive Surgery (RMIS)
* Demands repeated, precise manipulation of a curved needle under constrained instrument motion
* Automating components of this process could reduce procedural variability and operative fatigue
