The invention provides a sensor that detects a force acting around a rotation axis, a drive mechanism that incorporates this sensor, and a robot that uses the drive mechanism. It is intended for articulated (multi‑joint) robots used in industrial production lines, where precise force control is required to avoid damaging or deforming workpieces. 

Key technical aspects:

- The sensor is positioned to measure torque at the joint axis of a robot arm, enabling high‑accuracy drive control of the articulated robot.  
- It is especially needed when handling flexible, lightweight, or low‑strength members (e.g., flexible workpieces, low‑strength components) where only small forces can be applied without risking breakage.  
- In automobile‑part assembly lines, the robot typically handles loads ranging from several hundred grams to several kilograms; for delicate assembly tasks, loads can be as low as a few grams.  
- Because the torque sensor must provide precise measurements for fine‑grained force control, its resolution needs to be increased to accurately detect the torque generated by the drive mechanism.  
- The drive mechanism uses feedback from the torque sensor to regulate the force applied at the end effector and, optionally, at each link of the robot arm, facilitating precise fitting and assembly operations.
