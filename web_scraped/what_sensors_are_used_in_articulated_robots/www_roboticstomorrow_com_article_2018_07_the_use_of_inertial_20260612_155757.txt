**Key Technical Points on Inertial Sensors for Robotics (as described by LORD MicroStrain)**  

- **Primary robotic applications** – platform stability, global positioning, and increasingly as feedback for articulated robotic limbs.  

- **Critical performance specifications**  
  - **Dynamic roll‑pitch accuracy** – the most important spec for robotics; static accuracy and repeatability are usually listed on data sheets, but dynamic accuracy varies with the motion profile of each application. LORD recommends measuring in‑run error to evaluate a sensor’s true dynamic performance.  
  - **Latency** – essential for sensors that operate in closed‑loop balance or attitude control; lower latency improves feedback response.  
  - **Bias stability** – MEMS sensors can maintain good bias stability at a constant temperature, but bias can drift significantly with temperature changes.  
  - **Temperature‑related effects** – cycling temperature can cause bias and scale‑factor hysteresis, step jumps, and embedment stress (due to coefficient‑of‑thermal‑expansion mismatches in low‑cost packaging and mounts).  

- **Calibration and quality assurance**  
  - LORD has shipped **every inertial device since 2004 with a full calibration report**.  
  - Uses **four‑generation, proprietary, high‑volume NIST‑traceable calibration equipment**.  
  - Provides **ISO‑9001/AS9100‑certified** calibration and temperature‑compensation services, enabling low‑cost, high‑volume production.  

- **Product strategy for robotics**  
  - Offers a **catalog of COTS (commercial‑off‑the‑shelf) products** that can be selected by feature and performance, avoiding the need for customers to redesign hardware when newer components become available.  
  - Maintains **stable device footprints and APIs** for “drop‑in” upgrades, allowing seamless integration of newer MEMS technologies.  

- **Performance claims**  
  - LORD’s **Auto Adaptive EKF** (Extended Kalman Filter) has shown superior dynamic roll‑pitch accuracy in head‑to‑head tests against other EKFs.  
  - Customers report that LORD devices “just work better” despite comparable spec sheets, attributed to the company’s deep insight into extracting optimal dynamic performance from well‑behaving MEMS components.  

These points summarize the technical specifications, performance considerations, and calibration practices highlighted for inertial sensors used in modern robotic systems.
