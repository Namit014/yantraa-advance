An articulated robot isan industrial robot that possesses multiple articulated joints, allowing its arm to move in several directions much like a human arm. These joints give the robot a high degree of flexibility, enabling it to perform complex, varied tasks with precise control. The robot is equipped with advanced sensors (e.g., force sensors, vision systems) and is programmed through specialized software that defines the required movements, speeds, and tool operations. Its control system calculates joint angles, plans obstacle‑free paths, and powers the joints with electric motors or hydraulic actuators. Real‑time sensor feedback lets the robot adjust its motion, force, and speed while executing tasks such as assembly, material handling, painting, welding, or picking and placing objects. Safety features—including emergency stops, barriers, and safety sensors—protect operators.

**Key differences from other industrial robots**  
- **Flexibility:** Articulated robots have several joints, providing a wider range of motion compared with Cartesian, SCARA, or delta robots, which are typically limited to specific motion patterns.  
- **Application suitability:** Their versatility makes them ideal for tasks that require precise, multi‑directional movement, such as assembly, material handling, and complex machining.

**Typical applications**  
- **Assembly:** Fast, accurate placement and welding on production lines.  
- **Material handling:** Sorting, stacking, and moving objects in warehouses or factories.  
- **Painting & coating:** Applying consistent paint layers, especially in automotive manufacturing.  
- **Picking & placing:** Loading/unloading conveyor belts, organizing products on shelves, and automating repetitive pick‑place operations.

**Benefits**  
- **Improved precision** – reduces errors in assembly, welding, and painting.  
- **Higher productivity** – operates continuously without breaks, increasing output speed.  
- **Enhanced flexibility** – can be reprogrammed for new tasks, adapting quickly to production changes.  
- **Better safety** – built‑in sensors enable real‑time adjustments, and safety systems (e‑stops, barriers) protect humans.
