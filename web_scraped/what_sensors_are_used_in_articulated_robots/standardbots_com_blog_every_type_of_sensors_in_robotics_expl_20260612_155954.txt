Robotics sensors enable machines to sense and interpret their environments. Key sensor categories include:

- **Position and Motion Sensors**: Encoders, gyros; crucial for precise movement in arms and mobile robots.
- **Proximity Sensors**: IR sensors for short-range detection; ultrasonic sensors for distance; capacitive sensors for detecting nearby objects.
- **Vision and Camera Sensors**: 2D/3D cameras, depth sensors for object recognition; computer vision for navigation and inspection.
- **Force and Torque Sensors**: Measure pressure and rotational force; vital for grip control and delicate tasks.
- **Range Sensors**: Ultrasonic sensors (low cost); laser/LiDAR sensors (high accuracy) for obstacle avoidance and mapping.
- **Tactile and Touch Sensors**: Capacitive, pressure pads, artificial skin for contact feedback and precise manipulation.
- **Environmental Sensors**: Temperature sensors, gas detectors, humidity sensors, and air quality monitors for safe operation.

Together, these sensors allow robots to perform tasks accurately, adapt to dynamic conditions, and safely interact with humans and objects.
