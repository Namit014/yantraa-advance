# Articulated Robots: Technical Specifications and Overview

## Anatomy of Articulated Robots
- **Structure**: Composed of interconnected segments (links) connected through movable joints.
- **Joints**:
  - **Revolute joints**: Rotary joints allowing rotation along an axis.
  - **Prismatic joints**: Linear joints enabling straight-line movement.
- **Degrees of Freedom (DOF)**:
  - Range: 2 to 6 or more.
  - Common configurations: 4-DOF (3 revolute + 1 prismatic) and 6-DOF (3 revolute + 3 prismatic).
  - Higher DOF enables greater flexibility and precision.
- **End-effectors**: Tools attached to the robot arm for task-specific manipulation (e.g., grippers, suction cups).

## Control Systems
- **Types**:
  - **Open-loop systems**: Pre-programmed for simple, repetitive tasks without real-time feedback.
  - **Closed-loop systems**: Use sensors for real-time feedback and adjustments.
- **Sensors**:
  - Encoders, accelerometers, proximity sensors for position, orientation, and error detection.
- **Actuators**:
  - Electric motors (e.g., servo motors), hydraulic cylinders, pneumatic cylinders.
- **Interface**: Computer-based graphical user interface (GUI) for programming, parameter setting, and real-time performance monitoring.
- **Advancements**: Integration of AI and machine learning for adaptive learning and improved task flexibility.

## Advantages and Limitations
### Advantages:
- **Flexibility**: Versatile for diverse tasks across industries.
- **Precision**: High accuracy due to advanced control systems, sensors, and actuators.
- **Efficiency**: Faster and more productive than manual labor, reducing costs.
- **Safety**: Handles hazardous or dangerous tasks, reducing workplace risks.

### Limitations:
- **Cost**: High purchase and maintenance expenses, limiting accessibility for small businesses.
- **Programming Complexity**: Requires specialized expertise.
- **Maintenance**: Demands skilled personnel and equipment.
- **Space Requirements**: Large operational footprint.
- **Adaptability**: Struggles in unpredictable or rapidly changing environments.

## Applications
Articulated robots are deployed in:
- **Industries**: Automotive, aerospace, healthcare, manufacturing, and research.
- **Tasks**: Welding, painting, assembly, pick-and-place operations, and precision handling.
- **Environments**: Structured settings requiring repetitive or high-precision work.
