**Search and Rescue Robots**

* Designed for rescuing people
* Used to search, map, remove rubble, deliver supplies, provide medical treatment, and evacuate casualties
* Examples of uses:
	+ 9/11 attacks
	+ 2016 Central Italy earthquake
* Development projects:
	+ TRADR (User-Centric Design Methodology)
	+ SHERPA (mixed ground and aerial robotic platform)
	+ ICARUS (unmanned Search and Rescue devices)

**TRADR**

* Develops novel science and technology for human-robot teams
* Collaborative effort between robots and human team members
* Enables team to develop understanding of disaster area over multiple sorties

**SHERPA**

* Develops mixed ground and aerial robotic platform
* Address research topics on cognition and control in real-world hostile environment
* Focuses on interaction and collaboration between "busy genius" and SHERPA animals
* Emphasizes robust autonomy and cognitive capabilities

**ICARUS**

* Develops unmanned Search and Rescue technologies for detecting, locating, and rescuing humans
* Focuses on reducing time and increasing efficiency in Search and Rescue (SAR) process

**Research and Development**

* Biophysicist Daniel Goldman is building a robot inspired by sandfish lizards
* Associate Professor of Robotics-Howie Choset is working on building a snake robot
* Strong government funding and support is needed for widespread use of search and rescue robots
