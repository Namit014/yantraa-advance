**Role of Robots in Disaster Response:**

* Robots can operate in environments that are too dangerous or inaccessible for humans.
* They can navigate through rubble, enter unstable structures, and traverse hazardous terrains.
* Robots can quickly locate survivors in disaster zones or collapsed buildings using thermal imaging and sound detection.
* They can safely assess and mitigate risks in situations involving chemical spills or radiation.

**Real-World Examples:**

* 9/11 Response (2001): PackBot was deployed to navigate the rubble of the World Trade Center and provided valuable data and helped locate survivors.
* Hurricane Harvey (2017): Drones were used to survey flooded areas, locate stranded individuals, and deliver supplies.
* California Wildfires (2018): Drones were deployed to map affected areas, monitor fire spread, and assess damage.

**Future of Robotics in Disaster Response:**

* Advances in AI, machine learning, and robotics are paving the way for even more sophisticated machines.
* Swarms of autonomous drones working together to map disaster zones or humanoid robots capable of performing complex rescue operations in unstable environments are possibilities.
