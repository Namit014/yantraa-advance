**Types of Rescue Robots:**

* Unmanned Aerial Vehicles (UAVs) or drones
* Land robots (ground-based rescue vehicles)
* Water rescue robots (unmanned underwater vehicles (UUVs) or remotely operated vehicles (ROVs))

**Key Features and Technologies:**

* Artificial Intelligence (AI) for data analysis, decision-making, and task completion
* Sensors (e.g., cameras, thermal imaging, LiDAR, proximity sensors, gas sensors, acoustic sensors)
* Global Positioning System (GPS) for navigation and mapping
* Energy-dense batteries for extended operation

**Benefits of Using Rescue Robots:**

* Increased safety for human rescuers
* Enhanced efficiency and speed in search and rescue operations
* Ability to access hard-to-reach or hazardous areas
* Faster disaster response, leading to more lives saved

**Applications:**

* Earthquakes: Rescue robots can navigate through rubble and debris, identifying and reaching trapped victims
* Floods: Underwater robots can search for victims trapped below the water's surface
* Wildfires: Rescue robots built with heat-resistant materials can aid in firefighting scenarios

**Examples of Successful Rescue Missions:**

* Drone Helps in Search and Rescue Efforts After 7.8-Magnitude Earthquake in Syria and Turkey
* University of Illinois Students Develop Drone-Based System to Assess Structural Damage After 7.1-Magnitude Earthquake in Mexico City
* Hyrodnalix's Emergency Integrated Lifesaving Lanyard (EMILY) for rescue operations in water

**Notes:**

* Researchers are experimenting with robots modeled after snakes to aid in search and rescue operations
* The RoBoa, developed by ETH Zurich, has flexible parts and carries sensors, cameras, and speakers to navigate through rubble and debris
* Drones can be equipped with thermal cameras, multi-spectral cameras, or high-resolution photography to aid in assessment and damage mitigation
