**Autonomous Robot Project (RABOT) Overview**

**Project Highlights:**

* Design and development of a novel autonomous hybrid leg-wheeled robot with parallel mechanism for movement, allowing it to adapt to uncertain environments.
* Development of novel haptic interfaces using advanced control technologies, enabling human operators to feel the environment better and manipulate the slave robot intuitively.
* Multi-robot coordination control approach for enabling multiple robots to cooperate for rescue tasks.
* Six depth-of-field sensors for collecting information in disaster zones and optimizing movement.
* New hydraulic actuators for hexapod robots.

**Robots and Components:**

* Autonomous hybrid leg-wheeled robot with parallel mechanism for movement.
* Capsule robot for collecting information in disaster environments, along with wearable devices such as sensors.
* Wheelchair that can climb stairs, developed by Yanshan University.
* Robots with flexible leg-wheeled movement (three-degrees-of-freedom parallel leg mechanisms).
* Hexapod robots with new hydraulic actuators.

**Project Outcomes:**

*  Four patents concerning robot movement and haptic interfaces (one in the EU and three in China).
* Five awards for project work.
* EU funding through Marie Skłodowska-Curie actions programme enabled 77 exchange visits and direct training for about 270 people.
