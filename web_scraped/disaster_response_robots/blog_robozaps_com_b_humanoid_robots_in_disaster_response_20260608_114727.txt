**Key Statistics:**

* Search and rescue robotics market valued at over $27 billion in 2025 and projected to reach $70.33 billion by 2030 (14.79% CAGR)
* Quadrupling natural disaster frequency since 1970
* Rapid advances in AI autonomy

**Robot Categories:**

* Ground crawlers (snake robots, tracked vehicles)
* Aerial drones (thermal imaging, 3D mapping)
* Aquatic robots (flood rescue, underwater search)
* Quadrupeds (terrain traversal)
* Humanoid robots (door/valve operation, tool use, stair climbing)

**Purpose-Built Disaster Humanoids:**

* IIT's WALK-MAN (1.85 m, 102 kg, 33 DoF)
* Centauro hybrid (1.5 m, 93 kg, wheel-legged)
* Boston Dynamics Atlas

**Challenges:**

* Battery life limits most robots to 1-4 hours of operation
* Wireless communication fails inside collapsed structures
* Robots still require human supervision for complex life-or-death decisions

**Timeline:**

* 1995: Oklahoma City bombing and Kobe earthquake inspire the field of disaster robotics
* 2001: First robotic deployment at Ground Zero on September 11
* 2005: Aerial and aquatic drones used for damage assessment and survivor location in Hurricane Katrina
* 2011: iRobot PackBot and Warrior used in Fukushima Daiichi nuclear disaster
* 2013: DARPA unveils Atlas humanoid robot
* 2015: DARPA Robotics Challenge Finals, where Team KAIST's DRC-HUBO won
* 2016: IIT WALK-MAN disaster scenario test
* 2017: Carnegie Mellon snake robots deployed in Mexico City earthquake
* 2018: Centauro hybrid robot unveiled
* 2019: Shark Robotics' Colossus firefighting robot saved Notre-Dame Cathedral
* 2023: Turkey-Syria earthquake, where multiple drone and ground robot teams were deployed
* 2025: MIT SPROUT robot and CES 2025 humanoid surge
* 2026: Next-gen humanoids enter field testing

**DARPA Robotics Challenge:**

* 2012-2015: The single most important event in proving that humanoid robots could perform disaster response tasks
* 8 sequential tasks: Drive a vehicle, walk across rubble, open a door, turn a valve, cut through a wall, surprise task, climb stairs, cross uneven terrain
* DRC Task Requirements: Each robot had to complete 8 sequential tasks within 60 minutes

**Awards:**

* DRC Finals Results (June 2015): Top finishers in the DARPA Robotics Challenge Finals, where 25 humanoid robots from 5 countries competed in disaster tasks.
