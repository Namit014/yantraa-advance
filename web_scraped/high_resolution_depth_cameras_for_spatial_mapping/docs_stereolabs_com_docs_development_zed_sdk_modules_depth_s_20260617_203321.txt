============================================================

The ZED stereo camera functions similarly to human binocular vision, utilizing two horizontally separated lenses to capture a scene. By comparing the two images, it can estimate depth and 3D motion. Unlike indoor-only depth sensors, the ZED can capture depth up to 35 meters with a field of view of 110° (horizontal) by 95° (vertical).

Depth maps generated by the ZED store a distance value (Z) for each pixel (X, Y), measured in metric units from the camera's left lens. These maps require conversion to a color representation for visualization.

Alternatively, depth information can be represented as a 3D point cloud, which is a collection of (X,Y,Z) points representing the scene's surface, potentially including color data.

Depth accuracy in stereo vision diminishes with distance. The formula for depth resolution is given as $D_r = Z^2 \cdot \alpha$, where $D_r$ is depth resolution and $Z$ is the distance. For ZED cameras, depth accuracy is approximately 1% of the measured distance at close range, increasing to around 9% at the maximum range. Accuracy can be further reduced on surfaces lacking visual features, such as plain walls or reflective areas, due to temporal instability in measurements.
