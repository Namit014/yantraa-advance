The Teledyne Bumblebee X stereo camera can deliver high-accuracy disparity maps at up to 17.2 FPS at 2048×1536 resolution. It utilizes a Semi-Global Block Matching (SGBM) algorithm. SGBM struggles with textureless or reflective regions, producing gaps and incomplete disparity coverage.

Two deep learning approaches are presented to improve disparity accuracy and completeness:

1.  **Disparity Refinement (LingBot-Depth)**:
    *   Takes the on-board SGBM disparity and the left RGB image as inputs.
    *   Uses a Vision Transformer (ViT-Large) backbone conditioned on the initial depth map.
    *   Fills disparity gaps, sharpens edges, and reduces artifacts.
    *   Processes inputs including the left RGB image, SGBM-derived disparity converted to metric depth, and normalized camera intrinsics.
    *   Achieves approximately 5.9 FPS on an NVIDIA RTX 5070 Ti at 2048×1536 resolution.

2.  **End-to-End Deep Learning Stereo**:
    *   Estimates disparity directly from stereo image pairs.
    *   **Selective-Stereo**:
        *   Built on the IGEV-Stereo architecture with iterative GRU refinement.
        *   Employs adaptive frequency selection with high-frequency and low-frequency branches.
        *   Achieves approximately 0.67 FPS on an NVIDIA RTX 5070 Ti at 2048×1536 resolution.
    *   **FoundationStereo**:
        *   A foundation model developed by NVIDIA, using a DINOv2 vision transformer backbone.
        *   Employs a two-stage hierarchical inference strategy: a coarse pass at reduced resolution followed by a full-resolution refinement pass.
        *   Runs at approximately 0.21 FPS on an NVIDIA RTX 5070 Ti at 2048×1536 resolution.
