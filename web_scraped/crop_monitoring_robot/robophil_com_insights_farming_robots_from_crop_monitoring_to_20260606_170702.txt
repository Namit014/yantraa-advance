**Farming Robots:**

* Monitoring: capturing data about plant health, soil moisture, pest infestations, disease onset, nutrient stress, and more.
* Scouting: capturing NDVI, detecting water stress, disease hotspots, or pest damage.
* Weeding: using vision to distinguish weeds from crops and then mechanically removing weeds or applying spot herbicide only where required.
* Fertilization: analyzing soil nutrient levels and only depositing fertilizer where needed, in optimal amounts.
* Harvesting: requiring gentle handling, recognition of ripeness, and adaptability to variation in plant geometry.
* Examples: FarmWise (mechanical weeding), Swiss company Gamaya (hyperspectral drone cameras), and AHPPEBot (tomato harvesting robot).

**Technical Descriptions:**

* Agricultural sector transformation: robots are no longer sci-fi curiosities but real, practical tools helping farmers monitor crops, optimize inputs, and harvest produce.
* Benefit: precision farming, labor savings, reduced chemical usage and cost, and environmental impact.
* Limitation: deployment challenges, compatibility with existing workflows, and talent recruitment.
* Real-world examples: Swiss company Gamaya (hyperspectral drone cameras), FarmWise (mechanical weeding), and AHPPEBot (tomato harvesting robot).
