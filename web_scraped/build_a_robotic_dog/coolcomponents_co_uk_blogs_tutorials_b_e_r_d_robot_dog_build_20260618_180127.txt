## Legs Assembly (x4)

1. Slot the two lower leg sections into the foot.
2. Take two upper leg segments and place one between the lower legs, lining up the screw hole. Insert a 25mm screw and place the other upper leg segment on the other side, ensuring screws are inserted from the curved side of the foot.
3. Insert the Servo into the Upper Leg Servo Holder with the wire pointing toward the less curved side. Feed the wire through the gap, then slot the servo into place. Secure using one of the larger screws from the servo pack.
4. Connect the Servo Holder to the rest of the leg using the screw hole on the side the wire is on. Use a 20mm screw.
5. Using one hip piece, connect the other side of the servo holder to the higher screw hole on the hip, ensuring the servo holder piece is on the outside. Use a 16mm screw.
6. Connect the other side of the hip to the upper leg segments connected to the lower leg earlier. The hip segment sits between the two other leg segments. Secure with a nut on the inside of the leg using a 20mm bolt.

### Creating Holes in Servo Horn

The servo horn must rotate freely when attached to the servo lever to avoid strain and damage. A larger hole must be made in the servo horn using the included self-tapping screw (or a suitably sized drill bit under adult supervision if experienced).

**Using the included screw:**
1. Screw the self-tapping screw into the second hole from the end of the horn, only about halfway.
2. Unscrew, flip the horn over, and repeat from the other side.
3. This creates a larger hole in which the servo screw can sit and rotate freely.
4. Place the horn over the smaller hole on the leg lever and screw in the screw until the leg lever is securely held (do not fully screw in).

### Attaching the Servo Lever to the Leg Assembly

1. Place a 30mm screw through the lever, then add a 3mm spacer.
2. Place the screw through the free holes toward the bottom of the legs and secure using a nut.
3. Leave the servo horn unattached from the servo at this point — connect after calibrating the servos.

Repeat to create four legs total: two left legs and two right legs, using one of each hip type on both sides.

## Leg Servo Calibration

Before attaching servo horns, set all servos to a consistent value (position 90, the middle of the servo's range) so all legs behave similarly during programming.

**For BBC micro:bit with Motor:bit:**
1. Upload code to the BBC micro:bit that sets the servo position to 90 (digital write is included to stop servo buzzing).
2. Insert BBC micro:bit into the Motor:bit.
3. Connect a servo to the three pins labelled P3, matching wire colours to pin colours.
4. Press the A button to adjust the servo.
5. Repeat for remaining servos.

**For Kitronik Servo Driver:**
- Add the Kitronik I2C Servo Extension in MakeCode, then use the set servo block.

**For Arduino/Other Platforms:**
- Use the following code, replacing X with the appropriate pin number, and run on each servo before attaching horns:

```cpp
#include <Servo.h>
Servo myservo1;

void setup() {
  myservo1.attach(X);
}

void loop() {
  myservo1.write(90);
}
```

### Connecting Servo Horns After Calibration

Connect the servo horns to the servos in the position shown in the guide to ensure uniform leg behavior. The horns fit tightly and may need a slight push. Secure with the smallest screw included with each servo, using the center hole of the servo horn connector.

## Main Body: Lever Assembly

1. Attach servo horns (from the two remaining servos) to the levers that move the central body pieces. With both curved levers facing left, attach the servo horns by placing the screw through the hole and screwing it into the small hole on the curved end of the lever.
2. Attach three pieces together using 2 x 20mm bolts. The longer piece and shorter piece mount on either side of the square piece.
3. Using 2 x 16mm bolts and a 3mm spacer, connect the levers to the larger square board. Ensure the spacer is placed on the bolt above the lever. The longer lever connects to the notch on the side of the square; the other connects to the only other free hole. Both levers should curve toward each other.
