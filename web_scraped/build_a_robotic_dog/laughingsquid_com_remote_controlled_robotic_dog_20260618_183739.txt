# Remote-Controlled Robotic Dog Inspired by Theo Jansen's Strandbeest Design

A remote-controlled robotic dachshund was constructed by a science channel engineer, utilizing leg mechanics inspired by the mechanical design pioneered by Dutch artist Theo Jansen for his famous Strandbeest kinetic sculptures.

## Key Specifications and Details

- **Material**: The robotic dog was built primarily out of **plywood**.
- **Control Mechanism**: It was operated via a custom remote controller, which was itself designed in the shape of a small dachshund (wiener dog).
- **Locomotion**: The Jansen-inspired leg mechanism produced a distinctive **waddling gait**, which coincidentally mirrored the characteristic walking style of the dachshund dog breed.
- **Inspiration**: The leg design is based on Theo Jansen's **Strandbeest** kinetic sculptures, which are wind-powered walking structures originally created as evolving art pieces.

## Context

The project was presented as a creative engineering endeavor for those unable to own a real dog, offering a plywood robotic alternative. The build was featured by **The Q science channel**.
