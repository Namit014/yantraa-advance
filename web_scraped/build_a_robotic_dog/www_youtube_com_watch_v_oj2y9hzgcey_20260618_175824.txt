# How to Build Robot Dog Petoi Bittle | Step-by-Step Assembly Guide

## Overview

This is a step-by-step assembly guide for building the Petoi Bittle, a small programmable robot dog. The video walks through the entire build process from unboxing to a fully assembled and functional robot dog.

## What You'll Need

- Petoi Bittle kit (includes body frame, legs, servos, NyBoard, and accessories)
- Small Phillips-head screwdriver
- Petoi mobile app (for calibration and control)

## Assembly Steps

### Step 1: Unbox and Organize Parts
Lay out all components and identify each part: the main body frame, four leg assemblies, servo motors, NyBoard (main controller board), battery, and various screws and connectors.

### Step 2: Attach Servos to the Body Frame
Mount the servo motors into the designated slots on the main body frame. Secure them with the provided screws. These servos will control the movement of each leg joint.

### Step 3: Assemble the Legs
Connect the leg segments to the servos. Each leg has multiple joints, and each joint is driven by a servo. Ensure each leg is properly aligned and moves freely without obstruction.

### Step 4: Install the NyBoard
Mount the NyBoard (the main controller) onto the body frame. This board is the "brain" of Bittle, controlling all servo movements and running pre-programmed gaits and behaviors.

### Step 5: Connect Servo Wires to NyBoard
Route and connect each servo's wire to the corresponding port on the NyBoard. Follow the labeled ports to ensure each servo is connected to the correct channel.

### Step 6: Install the Battery
Place the battery in its designated compartment and connect it to the NyBoard. Ensure the battery is securely seated and the connection is firm.

### Step 7: Attach the Head and Tail (if applicable)
If your Bittle kit includes a head module or tail, attach these to the front and rear of the body frame.

### Step 8: Secure All Screws and Check Connections
Go over the entire assembly, tightening all screws and verifying that all wire connections are secure and nothing is loose.

### Step 9: Power On and Calibrate
Turn on Bittle using the power switch. Use the Petoi mobile app to connect to Bittle via Bluetooth and run the calibration routine. Calibration ensures each servo is properly centered and the robot dog can stand and walk correctly.

### Step 10: Test Basic Movements
After calibration, test basic commands through the app: standing, walking, sitting, and other pre-programmed actions. Verify that all four legs move in coordination.

## Tips

- **Take your time** with servo alignment — misaligned servos can cause uneven walking.
- **Keep wires tidy** to avoid snagging during movement.
- **Update firmware** through the Petoi app for the latest features and improvements.
- **Refer to the official Petoi documentation** for detailed diagrams and troubleshooting.

## Conclusion

Once assembled and calibrated, Petoi Bittle is a fully functional, programmable robot dog capable of walking, performing tricks, and being customized with new behaviors through coding.
