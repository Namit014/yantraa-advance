**Product Overview**
- Mini Pupper is an open-source ROS robot dog platform supporting ROS SLAM, Navigation, and OpenCV AI features with Lidar and camera sensors.
- Target price: under $500 USD.
- Mini Pupper 2 is a ROS2-based successor launched on Kickstarter.

**Hardware Specifications**
- 12 DOF (degrees of freedom) — the first consumer 12 DOF quadruped robot designed for mass production.
- All 12 servos are individually controlled via a PCA9685 controller.
- Custom "Mini Pupper servo" — a purpose-built 9g-class servo with high torque and high fidelity, developed after off-the-shelf hobbyist servos failed quality requirements.
- Uses metal threaded inserts (not purely 3D-printed joints) for durability and easy disassembly/reassembly.
- IMU integrated into the carry board to reduce wiring.
- Customized servo cable lengths for a cleaner internal build.
- Onboard power switch (no need to plug/unplug wires to power on/off).
- Battery charged without removal from the robot; onboard voltmeter provides low-battery alarm.
- HMI LCD display for animal-like expressions and user interaction — first consumer quadruped robot with an LCD for animal behaviors.

**Gait Performance**
- Supports multiple gaits: Trot (diagonal pairs), Pace (lateral pairs), and Bound (front and rear pairs).
- Gait performance depends on the 12-servo control loop time.

**Software & Sensors**
- ROS-based (Mini Pupper 2 supports ROS2).
- OpenCV support with OAK-D-Lite 3D camera module.
- Lidar sensor for SLAM and Navigation.
- Compatible with Raspberry Pi.
- PS4 joystick support included in the repository.

**Repository Structure**
- Branches: Doc, Legacy, Mangdang, PS4Joystick, PupperCommand, StanfordQuadruped, UDPComms.
- Installation involves flashing Ubuntu 22.04 preinstalled image to an SD card, cloning the repository, and running `pre_install.sh` and `install.sh` scripts.
- 87 commits in the repository history.

**Recognition & Endorsements**
- Endorsed by ROS, OpenCV, and Raspberry Pi.
- Won a Hackaday Prize.
- Featured on the first page of Kickstarter and Makuake.
- Workshop held at Amazon 2022 re:MARS and ROSCon 2022.
- Supported by Nathan Kau (Stanford Student Robotics) and MangDang Technology Co., Limited.
