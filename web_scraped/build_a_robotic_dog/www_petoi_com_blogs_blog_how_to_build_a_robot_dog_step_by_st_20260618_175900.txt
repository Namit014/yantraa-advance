# How to Build a Robot Dog: Step-by-Step DIY Guide

## Overview

This guide walks through building the Petoi Bittle, a programmable quadruped robot dog. The assembly process takes approximately 1–2 hours, though experienced builders can complete it in under an hour.

---

## Hardware Assembly

### Preparing for Assembly

Before beginning, ensure you have all components from the Bittle construction kit, including frame parts, motors, screws, and any required tools. Familiarize yourself with each part and its function.

### Assembling the Frame

1. **Build the main body** — Start with the body parts, fitting each piece snugly together. The frame serves as the foundation, so secure all connections tightly using the provided screws and tools.
2. **Attach the legs** — Each leg consists of multiple joints that allow fluid movement. Pay close attention to the orientation of each leg to ensure balanced movement when operational.

### Installing the Servo Motors

Bittle has **eight servos serving its limbs and one servo on its neck**, for a total of **nine servos and nine degrees of freedom (DoF)**. Carefully connect each motor to its respective leg joint, following the assembly guide instructions. Secure the motors firmly to prevent disconnections during operation.

### Wiring and Electronics

Connect the servo motors to the microcontroller, ensuring each wire is correctly aligned with its corresponding port. Proper wiring is essential for communication between the robot's components.

### Final Assembly Checks

After wiring, verify that the frame is stable, motors are securely attached, and wiring is tidy.

> For a detailed build with pictures of each part and screw, refer to the **Bittle Manual**.

---

## Software Setup

Every robot runs on a base system. The two popular robotics operating systems are:

- **ROS** (Robot Operating System)
- **OpenCat** — the open-source framework that powers Petoi Bittle, Bittle X, and robot cat Nybble

For small robots like Bittle, a full operating system isn't necessary — they run firmware on a microcontroller.

### Uploading and Configuring Firmware

1. **Connect** — Use a USB cable to link Bittle to your computer, ensuring correct drivers are installed.
2. **Upload the Firmware** — The easiest method is using the **Petoi Firmware Uploader** to upload OpenCat firmware. Alternatively, use the Arduino IDE for NyBoard or BiBoard: download the OpenCat source folder, open it in Arduino IDE, select the appropriate board and port, then build and upload.
3. **Configure and Test** — Adjust settings for motor control and communication protocols. Power on the robot to verify all movements and commands work as expected.

> For a step-by-step in-depth process, refer to the **Petoi Doc Center**.

---

## Calibration

Calibrating Bittle is essential to ensure proper movement and prevent servo collisions or damage.

1. Ensure all servos are connected correctly to the circuit board, with legs and head **not yet attached**.
2. Power on the robot by long-pressing the battery button until the blue LED lights up.
3. Enter **calibration mode**, which rotates all joints to their neutral positions.
4. Install the **head** by aligning it with the central axis and securing it to the neck servo.
5. Insert the **legs** into the servos while ensuring they remain at right angles to the torso.
6. Use the app to fine-tune positions: adjust the neck servo angle until the head faces forward, and align the legs using the provided L-shaped ruler.
7. Secure the joints with screws once calibration is complete.

> For a step-by-step calibration guide with pictures, refer to the **Petoi calibration documentation**.
