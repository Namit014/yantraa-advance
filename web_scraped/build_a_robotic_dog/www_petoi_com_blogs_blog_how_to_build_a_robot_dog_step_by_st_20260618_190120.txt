# How to Build a Robot Dog: Step-by-Step DIY Guide

## Overview

This guide walks through building the Petoi Bittle, a programmable quadruped robot dog. The assembly process takes approximately 1–2 hours, though experienced builders can complete it in under an hour.

---

## Hardware Assembly

### Preparing for Assembly

Before beginning, verify that all components from the Bittle construction kit are present, including frame parts, motors, screws, and any required tools. Familiarize yourself with each part and its function before starting.

### Assembling the Frame

1. **Construct the main frame** by fitting the body parts together snugly. The frame serves as the foundation, so secure all connections tightly using the provided screws and tools.
2. **Attach the legs** to the frame. Each leg consists of multiple joints that enable fluid movement. Pay close attention to the orientation of each leg to ensure balanced movement when operational.

### Installing the Servo Motors

Bittle has **eight servos serving its limbs and one servo on its neck**, for a total of **9 servos and 9 degrees of freedom (DoF)**. The number of servos determines the range of movements Bittle can perform.

1. Carefully connect each servo motor to its respective leg joint, following the assembly guide instructions.
2. Secure the motors firmly to prevent disconnections during operation.

### Wiring and Electronics

1. Connect the servo motors to the microcontroller, ensuring each wire is correctly aligned with its corresponding port.
2. Proper wiring is critical for communication between the robot's components.

### Final Assembly Checks

After wiring is complete, verify that:
- The frame is stable
- All motors are securely attached
- Wiring is tidy and organized

---

## Software Setup

### Robotics Operating Systems

Two popular robotics operating systems are:
- **ROS** (Robot Operating System)
- **OpenCat** — the open-source framework that powers Petoi Bittle, Bittle X, and robot cat Nybble

For small robots like Bittle, a full operating system is not necessary; the robot runs **firmware** on a microcontroller.

### Uploading and Configuring Firmware

1. **Connect** the robot to your computer via USB cable, ensuring correct drivers are installed.
2. **Upload the firmware** using one of two methods:
   - **Easy method:** Use the Petoi Firmware Uploader to upload OpenCat firmware.
   - **Advanced method:** Use the Arduino IDE for NyBoard or BiBoard — download the OpenCat source folder, open it in Arduino IDE, select the appropriate board and port, then build and upload.
3. **Configure and test** — adjust settings for motor control and communication protocols, then power on the robot to verify all movements and commands work as expected.

---

## Calibration

Calibrating Bittle is essential to ensure proper movement and prevent servo collisions or damage.

1. Ensure all servos are connected correctly to the circuit board. **Do not attach the legs and head yet.**
2. Power on the robot by long-pressing the battery button until the blue LED lights up.
3. Enter **calibration mode**, which rotates all joints to their neutral positions.
4. **Install the head** by aligning it with the central axis and securing it to the neck servo.
5. **Install the legs** by inserting them into the servos while keeping them at right angles to the torso.
6. **Fine-tune positions** using the Petoi app — adjust the neck servo angle until the head faces forward, and align the legs using the provided L-shaped ruler.
7. **Secure all joints with screws** once calibration is complete.

## Next Steps

Once assembly, firmware upload, and calibration are complete, Bittle is ready for programming and play. The robot supports multiple coding languages and robotics curricula, making it suitable for educators, parents, hobbyists, and aspiring roboticists.
