**Device Description**

* Type: Parallel robot with three arms connected to universal joints at the base
* Key feature: Use of parallelograms in the arms to maintain orientation of the end effector
* Compared to Stewart platform, delta robot maintains end effector orientation

**Design**

* Delta robot has 4 (or 6) degrees of freedom
* Kinematic chains connect base to end-effector
* Parallelograms restrict end platform movement to pure translation (X, Y, Z directions)

**Performance**

* High speed: up to 300 picks per minute
* High accelerations: up to 100 g (30-100 g)
* High precision: 0.45 m/s speeds, 215 m/s² accelerations, and 75 Hz repetition rates

**Variations**

* Delta with 6 degrees of freedom (Fanuc company)
* Delta with 4 degrees of freedom (Adept company)
* Pocketdelta (Asyril SA): 3-axis version for high-speed, high-precision applications
* Delta direct drive: 3 degrees of freedom with motor directly connected to arms
* Delta cube (EPFL university laboratory LSRO): monolithic design for ultra-high-precision applications
* Linear delta arrangements: using linear actuators for larger working volumes

**Applications**

* High-speed pick-and-place tasks
* Packaging industries (sorting, packing, and manipulating small consumer goods)
* Electronics industry (placement of components onto PCBs, wire bonding, and micro-assembly operations)
* Medical applications (neurosurgical positioning systems, haptic devices, and virtual reality input systems)
* Additive manufacturing (delta-style 3D printers)
