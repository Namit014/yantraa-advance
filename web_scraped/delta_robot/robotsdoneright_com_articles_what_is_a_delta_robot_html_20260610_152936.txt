**What is a Delta Robot?**

* A type of parallel-link robot with a parallelogram arm design
* Also known as spider robots due to their arm design

**Design and Features**

* Three to four carbon-fiber, lightweight arms extend downward from the main robot body
* Arms are jointed in the middle, causing them to bend inward and connect to a small tooling plate
* Each arm is connected to a motor located within the main robot body
* Motors coordinate movements of the arms in up and down motions
* Joint movements cause the tooling plate to move across X, Y, and Z axes, creating a cylindrical work envelope

**Speed and Acceleration**

* Designed for high speed and high acceleration
* Main weight of the robot is stationary, allowing for low inertia and fast operating speeds

**Applications**

* Ideal for assembly, dispensing, pick and place, material handling, part transfer, and packaging robotic applications
* Suitable for lightweight parts with simple geometries
* Can be integrated with additional robotic technology such as force sensors and vision

**Specifications**

* Payload capacities:
	+ M-1ia: 1 kg
	+ M-2ia: wider work envelope
* Speeds:
	+ Up to 300 parts per minute
* Throughput rates:
	+ Dramatically high throughput rates

**Industry Use**

* Deployed in food, electronic, medical, and pharmaceutical industries for lightweight assembly processes
* Capable of increasing productivity and reducing cycle times
