**Overview**

* Type: Parallel robot
* Consists of: Three articulated arms connected to a fixed base via universal joints and to a mobile platform via spherical joints
* Enables: High-speed, precise translational motion in three dimensions (x, y, z) for manipulating lightweight objects (typically under 10 kg)

**Design and Operation**

* Employed parallelogram linkages in the arms to maintain the end-effector's orientation parallel to the base
* Provide three degrees of freedom for translation while minimizing inertia (often better than 0.1 mm)
* Actuation: Base-mounted motors (rotational servo motors) driving the linkages to transmit force to the mobile platform
* Minimizes inertia of moving parts, enabling rapid accelerations
* Motion constraints enforced by parallelogram linkages in each arm, restricting the mobile platform to pure translational movement in three dimensions

**Key Components**

* Arm linkages: Upper arms robust for torque transmission, lower arms employ lightweight materials (such as carbon fiber composites) to minimize inertia
* Lower arms: Lightweight materials to minimize inertia
* Mobile platform: Connects to lower arms through spherical joints, allowing for smooth articulation

**Performance**

* Achieves accelerations over 15 g and operation rates exceeding 200 cycles per minute in modern variants
* Provides high-speed operations compared to serial manipulators
* Achieves enhanced speed and repeatability, often better than 0.1 mm

**History**

* Invented by Reymond Clavel, Swiss engineer, in 1985 at École Polytechnique Fédérale de Lausanne (EPFL)
* Initial prototype developed in 1985 as part of Clavel's PhD research on parallel kinematic structures
* Design emphasized pure translational motion in three dimensions for enhanced precision
* Commercialization began in 1987 with demand for high-speed packaging tasks in the food industry
* First major commercial product: ABB IRB 340 FlexPicker, launched in 1998, achieving accelerations up to 10g
