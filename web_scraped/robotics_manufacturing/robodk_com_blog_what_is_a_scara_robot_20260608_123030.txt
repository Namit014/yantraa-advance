**Definition and Background**

* SCARA robots are a type of industrial robotic arm with selective compliance assembly robot arm (SCARA) technology.
* They are known for their ability to move freely and maintain stiffness in three axes while being compliant in the final axis.
* SCARA robots are commonly used in various industries, including manufacturing and assembly applications.

**History of SCARA Robots**

* SCARA robots were invented by Professor Hiroshi Makino of Yamanashi University in 1977.
* The first prototype was tested in 1978, and a second version was released two years later.
* The first commercial SCARA robot was released in 1981.

**Technical Specifications**

* SCARA robots usually feature 4 axes, with 2 parallel arms that can move in a single plane.
* The final axis is at right angles to the others and is compliant.
* They have a simple design, which allows for quick movement while maintaining precision and accuracy.
* SCARA robots are easy to program due to their simple inverse kinematics.

**Advantages and Disadvantages**

* Advantages:
	+ Fast cycle times
	+ Impressive positioning accuracy
	+ High repeatability
	+ Easy to program
* Disadvantages:
	+ Limited payload capacity (approximately 30-50 kg)
	+ Restricted workspace
