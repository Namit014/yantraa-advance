**Technical Specifications:**

* Four axes: two rotary joints for radial and transverse motion, a prismatic joint for vertical travel, and a rotational wrist for orientation
* Selective compliance in the X-Y plane, allowing flexibility in horizontal movements
* Rigidity in the Z-axis for precise vertical positioning
* Ability to operate at high speeds and accurate operations in assembly and material handling
* Payloads: from 1 to 20 kg
* Reaches: up to 1 meter
* Cycle times: critical in industries like electronics, automotive, and pharmaceuticals
* Precision: down to micron levels

**Key Advantages:**

* Compact footprint
* Cylindrical work envelope suited for benchtop integration
* Simplified programming via inverse kinematics for the two primary arm joints
* High-speed and accurate operations in assembly and material handling
* Adaptability across small-scale assembly to high-volume packaging lines

**Related Concepts:**

* Parallelogram linkage: a structural element in SCARA designs where two parallel links connected by revolute joints maintain constant orientation of the end-effector during horizontal motion
* Cartesian robots: use orthogonal linear axes for movement in a rectangular workspace
* Delta robots: parallel kinematic structures using multiple arms connected via parallelogram linkages to achieve high-speed planar motion
* Tricept robots: hybrid variants integrating a parallel three-degree-of-freedom platform with a serial wrist

**History:**

* Invention: 1978 by Professor Hiroshi Makino at Yamanashi University in Japan
* First prototype: developed in 1978
* Second prototype: developed in 1980, refining the design for improved performance
* Patent: filed by Makino in 1979 (granted in 1982)
* Initial testing: focused on electronic component assembly, demonstrating the robot's effectiveness in tasks such as placing parts on printed circuit boards
* Commercial adoption: began in 1981, with industrial partners such as Sankyo Seiki, Hirata, Pentel, and NEC marketing their own versions of SCARA-type robots
* Early commercial models: released in 1983, such as the Epson SSR-H series and Yamaha Motor's YAMAHA SCARA robot
