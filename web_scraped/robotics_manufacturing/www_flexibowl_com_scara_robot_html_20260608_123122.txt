**FlexiBowl:**

* Enables flexible feeding of entire families of parts
* Does not require retooling cost for product changeovers
* Feeds parts in a matter of seconds
* Combines servomotor and impulse generator for non-vibratory feeding
* Can feed parts of all sizes
* Replaces other parts feeders in industries such as automotive, electronics, and cosmetics

**Scara Robot Configuration:**

* 4-axis SCARA robot
* Inner link rotates about the World Z-axis
* Outer link rotates about a Z elbow joint
* Wrist axis moves up and down and rotates about Z

**Advantages of SCARA Robots:**

* Can operate at higher speed and with optional cleanroom specifications
* Can achieve tolerances lower than 10 microns
* Compact layout makes them easily re-located in temporary or remote applications
* Suitable for applications with a smaller field of operation and where floor space is limited

**Limitations of SCARA Robots:**

* Typically only capable of carrying a lighter payload (up to 2 kg nominal, 10 kg maximum)
* Limited dexterity and flexibility compared to six-axis robots
* Circular envelope does not suit all applications

**How a SCARA Robot Works:**

* 4-axis manipulator with motion in the X-Y and Z planes and 360-degree rotational movement about the Z-axis
* Uses inverse kinematics and data interpolation to move dynamically, quickly, and intelligently

**When to Use a SCARA Robot:**

* For pick-and-place or assembly operations when high speed and high accuracy are required
* For flexible assembly operations, such as in the automotive and electronics industries

**Why SCARA Robots are Preferred for Assembly Operations:**

* Provides substantial rigidity for the robot in the vertical direction, but flexibility in the horizontal plane
* Ideal for many assembly tasks, such as in the automotive and electronics industries
