**What is a SCARA Robot?**

* A type of industrial robot
* Originally developed in 1978 for assembly applications, now used for various tasks

**Specifications:**

* Small industrial robots, typically pedal-mounted or floor-mounted, with some wall-mounted models (e.g., FANUC SR series)
* Low payloads: 0.5 kg to 20 kg
* Reach: typically 1,000 mm or less
* Four-axis configuration (standard, with some three-axis models):
	+ X, Y, and Z axes: dynamic and rigid movements
	+ Z axis: fixed, rigid movement
* 360 degrees of rotational movement around the Z axis (cylindrical work envelope)

**Advantages:**

* Fastest type of robot based on four-axis configuration
* Best price-to-performance ratio due to fewer axes, smaller size, and reduced component and material usage
* Affordable alternative: buying used FANUC SR-6ia scara robots can be a cost-effective option

**Applications:**

* Assembly automation, including:
	+ High-speed assemblies (e.g., FANUC SR-12ia excels at assembly tasks)
* Material handling processes:
	+ Pick and place
	+ Packaging
* Other applications:
	+ 3D printing
	+ Inspection
	+ Palletizing
	+ Dispensing
