## Core Components

- **Frame**: The skeleton of the drone; must be rigid and strong.
- **Motors & Propellers**: Propulsion system; motors generate thrust, propellers create lift.
- **Electronic Speed Controllers (ESCs)**: Translators between flight controller and motors; deliver precise power to control motor speed.
- **Flight Controller (FC)**: The brain; takes radio stick commands and uses onboard sensors to keep the drone stable.
- **Power System**: Battery and Power Distribution Board (PDB); PDB distributes correct voltage from the battery to all electronics.

---

## Frame Materials
- **Carbon fiber**: Carbon fiber offers high rigidity and durability, suited for freestyle and racing drones.
- **Plastic**: Cheaper and lighter, suited for smaller indoor drones or micro builds; less stiff, can introduce vibrations.

## Frame Sizes (measured diagonally motor-to-motor in millimeters)
- **3-inch**: Suited for smaller, agile drones flown in parks or backyards.
- **5-inch**: Standard for FPV freestyle; balance of agility, power, and stability.
- **7-inch and larger**: Built for long-range cruising and efficiency; carries larger batteries, less nimble than smaller frames.

---

## Motors and KV Rating
The KV rating indicates how fast a motor spins (in RPM) per volt applied (without a propeller attached).

- **Low KV motors (1700–1900KV for a 5-inch build)**: Slower spinning, higher torque; suited for bigger props, heavier payloads, or efficient cinematic flight.
- **High KV motors (2400–2700KV for a 5-inch build)**: Raw speed and responsiveness; suited for racing and aggressive freestyle.

## ESC Configuration
- **4-in-1 ESC board**: Combines all four controllers onto a single board; cleaner build, less wiring/soldering; if one ESC fails, the entire board requires replacement.
- **Individual ESCs**: Easier field repair; a single failed unit can be swapped independently.
