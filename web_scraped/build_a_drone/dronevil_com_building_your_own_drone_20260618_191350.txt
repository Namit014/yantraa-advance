**Build Your Own Drone: Technical Overview**

This guide provides a practical step-by-step approach to constructing a custom drone, emphasizing component selection, tools, and assembly principles.

**Market & Basics**
- Quadcopters constitute approximately 90% of the drones market.
- Frame sizes:
  - Micro: 2–3 inch propellers (indoor/small spaces).
  - Mini: 3–4 inch.
  - Standard: 5–6 inch (balance of speed and control).
  - Large: 7+ inch (heavy payloads).

**Frame Materials**
- Carbon fiber frames offer the best strength-to-weight ratio, durability, and crash resistance.
- Alternatives include aluminum (stronger but heavier), glass fiber (lighter and cheaper), and 3D-printed plastic (affordable but less durable).

**Motor Selection**
- Brushless motors are preferred over brushed versions for efficiency and longevity.
- Motor sizes are denoted by a four-digit code: e.g., 2205 = 22 mm diameter, 5 mm height.
- kV rating indicates motor speed per volt (higher kV is faster, lower is more torque).
- Thrust-to-weight ratios:
  - Racing drones: 5:1.
  - Photography drones: 3:1 to 4:1.
  - General purpose: 10:1.

**ESC (Electronic Speed Controller)**
- ESCs control motor speed and direction for stability.
- Brushless motors require brushless ESCs; brushed motors do not.
- ESC protocols (e.g., PWM, Oneshot125, Multishot, Dshot) affect communication speed between the flight controller and motors.

**LiPo Batteries**
- LiPo batteries power drone systems; specifications include cell count, capacity, and discharge rate.

**Tools for Assembly**
- Soldering iron and solder for electronics assembly.
- Multimeter for diagnosing voltage, continuity, and resistance.
- Hex keys and drivers for frame assembly; ensuring correct hex screw sizes is critical.
- Additional tools: helping hands, wire cutters, specialty pliers, heat shrink, cable ties, double-sided tape, standoffs, thread locker.

This guide covers the essentials for building a functional custom drone, focusing on component selection, sizing, and practical best practices.
