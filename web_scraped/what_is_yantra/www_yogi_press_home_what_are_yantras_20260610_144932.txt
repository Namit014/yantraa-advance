**Yantra Definitions and Concepts**

* A Yantra is a tool designed to stimulate the dormant centers in the brain and expand consciousness (formed by the Sanskrit roots "yan" and "tra", meaning instrument and freedom/liberation, respectively).
* The human body is considered the most refined Yantra.
* Yantras are used to manifest inherent capabilities and destroy mental obstacles that hinder happiness and wellbeing.

**Yantra Designs and Shapes**

* Concentric figures, including triangles, squares, circles, stars, and lotuses, are used to create Yantras.
* The shapes can start at the center and emanate outward or at the edges and grow inward, symbolizing the progressive movement from unity to multiplicity and back again.
* Specific shapes and their meanings:
	+ Triangle (Trikona): upward-pointing triangle represents consciousness (male principle), downward-pointing triangle represents energy (female principle).
	+ Square (Bhupura): represents the earth element and the material world.
	+ Circle (Chakra): represents perfection, a creative void, and energy.
	+ Star (Satkona): has 6 points and symbolizes harmony in the male and female principles (equilibrium, balance, and unity of creation).
	+ Lotus (Padma): represents virtue, diversity, and freedom.
	+ Black Dot (Bindu): represents intense concentration, wholeness, and the creative void (nirvana concept).

**Yantra and Mantra Combinations**

* The combined use of Yantras and mantras can aid in spiritual progress along the spiritual path.
* Only certain masters can prescribe the adequate Yantra and mantra for an aspirant.
* Using Yantras and mantras recklessly can have adverse effects.

**Benefits of Yantra Meditation**

* Get rid of resentment, negative energy, and insufficiency.
* Attract wellbeing, prosperity, and abundance.
* Boost and support the flow of wealth.
* Maintain internal harmony and a sense of calmness.
* Attain a sense of belonging and oneness with the universe.

**Yantra Meditation Techniques**

* Hang the Yantra on a wall or place it on a desk facing the North or East, with the center of the Yantra at eye-level.
* Sit in Siddhasana or Vajrasana, with a straight spine and gentle breathing.
* Gaze directly at the center of the Yantra (Bindu) and then expand your vision to take in the whole design.
* Focus on the shapes in the Yantra, including triangles, circles, stars, and lotuses, and then slowly return to the center.
* Close your eyes and relish in the fact that you are one with the universe.

**Sri Yantra**

* The Sri Yantra is the most powerful of Yantras, representing the process of manifestation of energy from the dormant state, symbolizing the entire process of Universal evolution.
