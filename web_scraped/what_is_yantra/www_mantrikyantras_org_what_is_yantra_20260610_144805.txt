**Definition**: Yantra is a Sanskrit word that means "loot, instrument, or machine."

**Principles**: A Yantra is a mixture of three important principles: the form (Akriti-rupa) principle, the function (Kriya-rupa) principle, and the power (Sakthi-rupa) principle.

**Shapes**: Graphic shapes used in a Yantra correspond to the psychological schema. The outer gates, lotus petal, inner geometric shapes, and bindu represent the gates of one's consciousness, spiritual enfoldment, stages of spiritual ascent, and one's innermost self.

**Function**: Yantras function as aids to or chief instruments of meditative discipline, revealing the inner basis of the forms and shapes flourished in the universe.

**Squares**: Most Yantras are surrounded by a square (bhupur) with four gates, which represent the material world.

**Shapes in a Yantra**: A Yantra consists of a geometrical pattern made of several concentric figures such as circles, squares, lotuses, triangles, and points.

**Dot or Bindu**: The Dot or Bindu symbolizes the intense concentration and the origin of all that exists.

**Triangle**: The triangle, pointing down, represents the Yoni, the feminine genital organ, and the symbol of the supreme source of the universe. When pointing upwards, it signifies intense spiritual aspiration and the sublimation of one's nature into the most subtle planes.

**Shakti Kona**: The downward-pointing triangle is known as Shakti Kona, symbolizing the element of water (Jal Tattva) and the core of creation.

**Chakra**: The Chakra (Circle) stands for rotation, which is central to the macrocosmic progression, and signifies perfection along with the powerful creative void of the Vishudha Chakra.

**Bhupura**: The Bhupura (Square) is the exterior limit of the Yantra, representing the element earth (Prithvi Tattva).

**Padma (Lotus)**: The Padma (Lotus) symbolizes both purity and variety, and represents freedom from multiple interference with exterior purity, expressing the absolute force of the supreme self.

**Creation**: Yantras are created by the amalgamation of various geometric forms and patterns that teach the mind the power of concentration and focus.

**Energizing**: Yantras are created under definite rules and with specific rituals on specific divisions of time in a particular place using certain objects, symbols, sounds, names, and forms. They are energized through a 'Siddha Mantra' with utmost skill and earnestness.

**Prana Pratishta**: An elaborate ritual called "Prana Pratishta" is enacted to infuse the deity into the Yantra, involving mantras, flowers, pranayama practices, incense, and herbs.

**Usage**: Yantras can be worn or installed anywhere, except on Wednesday, Saturday, or amavasi, for the first time. It is not mandatory to be vegetarian while using Yantra, and women can wear or use Yantra during menstruation periods.

**Meditation**: To meditate, hang the Yantra on a wall facing north or East, placing the center of the Yantra at eye level, and adopt a favorite posture while maintaining a straight spine.
