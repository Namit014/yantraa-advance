Find sources: "Polish football clubs in European competitions" – news · newspapers · books · scholar · JSTOR ( April 2021 ) ( Learn how and when to remove this message ) Football clubs from Ekstraklasa – the top Polish professional league for men's association football teams – have participated in European football competitions since 1955–56 season, when Gwardia Warsaw took part in the inaugural European Cup . [ 1 ] The biggest success by Polish clubs in UEFA -administered competitions is reaching a semifinal of the European Cup . The first one to achieve this round was Legia Warsaw in the 1969–70 season , followed by Widzew Łódź in the 1982–83 season . Moreover, Górnik Zabrze reached the final of the 1969–70 European Cup Winners' Cup , as did Ruch Chorzów in the 1998 UEFA Intertoto Cup . [ 2 ] Polish clubs were more successful in non- UEFA -administered Intertoto Cup . Polonia Bytom won the 1964–65 edition followed by losing in the final next year. Odra Opole and Zagłębie Sosnowiec reached semi-finals, each once. From 1967 to 1994 , when only a group stage was held, 14 different clubs won their groups 24 times.
