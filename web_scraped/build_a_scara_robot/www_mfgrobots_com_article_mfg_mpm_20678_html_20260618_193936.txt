Technical specifications and descriptions:

The SCARA robot features 4 degrees of freedom, driven by 4 NEMA 17 stepper motors, with a small servo motor (MG996R) controlling the end effector (gripper). The control system consists of an Arduino UNO board, a CNC shield, and four A4988 stepper drivers. A custom Graphics User Interface was developed using the Processing environment, supporting both Forward and Inverse Kinematics control simultaneously on the same screen. Forward Kinematics enables manual joint angle adjustment via sliders, with real-time display of the end effector's X, Y, and Z values. Inverse Kinematics allows setting a desired end effector position, with automatic calculation of required joint angles. The program includes a "Save" button to record individual movements and a "Run" button to execute stored sequences continuously. Movement speed and acceleration can be adjusted via the interface.

The 3D model was created using 3DEXPERIENCE SOLIDWORKS (a cloud-based version of SOLIDWORKS accessible via the 3DEXPERIENCE platform). The platform includes additional apps such as Project Planner (for task organization and deadline tracking), 3D Markup (for viewing, exploring, and noting models on various devices), and SOLIDWORKS xDesign (a browser-based 3D modeler). The 3D model and STL files are available on Thangs. Most parts are designed to be 3D printable on printers with smaller beds (e.g., the Ender3), using PLA+ (blue) for most parts and normal PLA for pulleys and the gripper.

Reduction ratios for the joints (achieved via custom-designed parametric GT2 pulleys and closed-loop GT2 belts): the first joint has a 20:1 ratio across two stages using 200mm and 300mm belts; the second joint has a 16:1 ratio using the same two-stage method; the third joint has a 4:1 ratio with single-stage reduction. The GT2 pulleys themselves are 3D printable with parametric design for adjusting tooth count. Each joint incorporates two thrust bearings and one radial bearing. All joints are hollow to allow wiring passthrough for motors and micro switches. Slots for idler pulleys are included on each belt path for tensioning. The Z-axis uses an 8mm lead screw, while the entire arm assembly slides on four 10mm smooth rods with linear ball bearings. The smooth rods are 400mm in height, and the lead screw must be 398mm (2cm shorter) to fit within this configuration, unless spacer nuts are used to raise the Z motor by 2cm.

Print time totaled approximately 120 hours at a speed of 60mm/s, with the base taking around 32 hours. A horizontal expansion of -0.1mm was applied in the slicing software to improve dimensional accuracy and fit with mechanical components.

Assembly components include: 4x smooth rods (10mm diameter, 400mm length), 1x lead screw (8mm, 400mm), 4x linear bearings (10mm), 1x thrust ball bearing (40x60x13mm), 2x thrust ball bearings (35x52x12mm), 5x radial ball bearings (8x22x7mm), and various M3, M4, and M5 bolts and nuts. Assembly begins with the base, inserting a radial ball bearing (35mm inner, 47mm outer diameter), followed by a thrust bearing (40mm inner, 60mm outer diameter) between the pulley and base. The opposite side uses another identical thrust bearing with a joint coupler. The pulley and upper part are coupled using four M4 bolts (55mm length) with self-locking nuts. The middle pulley is paired with a 300mm GT2 belt, mounted using two 608 ball bearings (top and bottom of the base), secured with a 45mm M8 bolt, washer, and self-locking nut.
