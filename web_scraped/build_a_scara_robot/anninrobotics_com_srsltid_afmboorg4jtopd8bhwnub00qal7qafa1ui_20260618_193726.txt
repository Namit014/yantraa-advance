**Annin Robotics AR4 – Technical Overview**

The AR4 is a low-cost, open-source, 6-degree-of-freedom (6DOF) industrial robotic arm designed for desktop use. It is intended to be user-buildable, with parts machinable from aluminum or 3D-printable.

**Key Specifications & Features:**
- **Axes:** 6DOF
- **Control:** Arduino-based, programmable via Python or ROS (Robot Operating System)
- **Integration:** Supports Modbus protocol; compatible with PLCs, pneumatic grippers, and 7th-axis tracks
- **Customization:** Open-source design allows for code modification and 3D-printed custom parts
- **Applications:** Light assembly, CNC machine tending, R&D prototyping, vision-guided tasks (e.g., dish loading, label printing), custom workflows with G-code integration

**Target Users:**
- **Education:** Deployed in high schools, colleges, STEM classrooms, and university labs for teaching robotics, automation, and coding.
- **Industry & Makers:** Used by workshops, startups, and creative professionals for affordable automation, packaging, sample processing, and light production tasks.

**Design Philosophy:** Bridges DIY projects and professional automation, offering plug-and-play integration and flexibility for real-world applications.
