The LoaD project is an eight‑year research program (2022–2030) that investigates optimal joint loading for people with knee osteoarthritis. It is a collaborative effort involving university hospitals, technical universities, colleges, patient organisations, sports federations and companies.

Key elements of the project:

- **Target population:** Active individuals with knee osteoarthritis.
- **Study design:** Longitudinal follow‑up of participants for two years.
- **Conducting institutions for one sub‑study:** Department of General Practice, Erasmus MC and Rotterdam University of Applied Sciences.
- **Purpose:** To determine the amount and type of joint loading that is most beneficial for maintaining function and reducing progression of knee osteoarthritis.
- **Stakeholders:** Participants, healthcare providers, and researchers.
