**Load Projects – Technical Overview**

- **Definition** – Large‑scale facilities that cause a massive increase in electric demand and require high‑voltage, 24/7 power connections.  
- **Typical Capacity Requirements** – 100 MW – 1 GW+ for hyperscale data centers; 300 MW+ for other industrial loads.  

### The “Big Four” Drivers of Load Growth
| Category | Typical Applications | Power Demand |
|----------|----------------------|--------------|
| Hyperscale Data Centers | AI and cloud computing facilities | 100 MW – 1 GW+ |
| Advanced Manufacturing | Semiconductor fabs, battery gigafactories, hydrogen hubs | 100 MW – 500 MW |
| EV Charging Infrastructure | High‑density fleet depots (trucks, buses) | 50 MW – 200 MW |
| Industrial Decarbonization | Steel, cement, other heavy industries moving to electric‑arc furnaces or green‑hydrogen processes | 200 MW – 500 MW |

### Interconnection Queue Bottlenecks
- **Regions with longest waits:** PJM (Northern Virginia/Mid‑Atlantic) and ERCOT (Texas).  
- **Typical wait times for large loads:** 3 – 7 years to energization.  
- **Impact:** A single 500 MW load can consume all available capacity at a substation, blocking other developers for many miles.

### LandGate Load‑Project Intelligence (Key Data Offerings)

1. **Real‑Time Offtake Capacity & Feeder Headroom**  
   - Coverage: > 21,000 Points of Interconnection (POIs).  
   – Enables instant assessment of available capacity at specific substations or transmission lines.  
   – Reduces reliance on costly utility feasibility studies (≈ $50 k, 6‑month lead time).

2. **Load Queue Competitive Intelligence**  
   - GIS‑mapped load interconnection queues.  
   - Shows pending load requests, Interconnection Agreement (IA) status, and projected energization dates.  
   - Helps avoid oversaturated clusters and identify emerging markets (e.g., Midwest, Southeast).

3. **Proximity to Generation & Behind‑the‑Meter (BTM) Strategy**  
   - Overlay of load sites with existing generation assets (nuclear, gas, solar, wind).  
   - Supports co‑location modeling for BTM power arrangements, allowing facilities to draw directly from nearby plants and bypass utility constraints.

4. **Nodal Pricing and LMP Forecasting**  
   - Historical and forecasted Locational Marginal Pricing (LMP) at node level.  
   - Example impact: a $0.01/MWh difference for a 300 MW facility translates to several million dollars in annual operating expenses.

5. **Automated Due‑Diligence – Data Center Feasibility Report**  
   - One‑click compilation of: off‑take capacity, fiber‑optic proximity, water availability, and environmental‑risk ratings.  
   - Shrinks due‑diligence timelines from weeks (consultant‑driven) to seconds (automated).

### Operational Implications
- **Power‑First Real Estate:** Site selection now prioritizes grid capacity (“right to pull energy”) over land cost.  
- **Strategic Shift:** Developers must evaluate off‑take capacity, queue position, and nodal pricing early to de‑risk multi‑hundred‑MW investments.  
- **Cost Sensitivity:** Small variations in LMP (e.g., $0.01/MWh) can affect OPEX by millions of dollars for large loads.  

These specifications and data capabilities form the technical foundation for planning, siting, and executing large‑scale load projects in today’s rapidly evolving U.S. electric grid.
