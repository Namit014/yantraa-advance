**Robotic Polishing Systems**

* Consist of:
	+ A 6-axis or SCARA robot
	+ End-of-arm tooling (abrasive pads, wheels, belts)
	+ Force control or compliance mechanisms
	+ Vision or laser sensors for surface mapping
	+ Integration with conveyors, jigs, or parts feeders
* The robot follows programmed paths across the surface of a part, applying uniform pressure and motion to achieve the desired finish.

**Impact of Robotic Polishing in Industry**

* Replaces or supplements manual polishing, improving:
	+ Output quality
	+ Operator safety
	+ Efficiency
* Achieves consistent surface quality by following precise, repeatable paths under controlled conditions.
* Increases throughput by:
	+ Polishing continuously without rest, fatigue, or slowdown
	+ Switching between polishing steps
	+ Adjusting pressure on the fly
	+ Performing multi-pass operations
* Reduces operator exposure to: airborne particles, repetitive strain, noise, and physical fatigue.
* Real-time force sensors, compliance units, or machine vision adjust pressure and path in response to surface geometry.

**Material Versatility**

* Robots can polish a wide range of materials, including:
	+ Stainless steel and aluminum
	+ Plastic housings and enclosures
	+ Glass panels or optical components
	+ Fiberglass and carbon fiber composites
	+ Tooling and programming can be tailored for each material type.

**Real-World Applications**

* Automative: polishing wheels, trim, exhaust tips, or body panels
* Consumer Products: achieving high gloss finishes
* Aerospace: polishing turbine blades and composite surfaces
* Medical Devices: smoothing surgical instruments or implants
* Luxury Goods: perfecting the polish on watches, electronics, or designer hardware

**Programming and Automation Flexibility**

* Robotic polishing routines can be:
	+ Taught manually using lead-through programming
	+ Developed in simulation software
* Adjustments can be made for new part geometries, wear compensation, or finishing sequences without major hardware changes.
* Offline programming tools allow manufacturers to validate polishing paths in advance, ensuring minimal setup time and zero scrap during testing.
