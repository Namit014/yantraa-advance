**Assessing the Current Polishing Process**

* Define polishing process that is repetitive and consistent
* Need at least 1 meter of open space for a robot work cell
* Consider the desired reach (or arm length) of the robot
* Consider the amount of force the robot can apply, especially for polishing rough materials
* Precision (repeatability) is an important factor for polishing small or intricate parts

**Safety First: Risk Assessments and Safeguarding for Robotic Polishing**

* Conduct a risk assessment to identify any dangers related to implementing a robot
* Consider hazards such as:
	+ Places where body parts could get caught on an end-effector
	+ Heavy loads that require safe lifting equipment
	+ Hazards specific to the industry
	+ Sharp edges and abrasives
	+ Wiring or hoses
	+ High voltages
* Consider minimizing or removing the need for staff interactions with the robot
* Implement safeguards:
	+ Use barriers like fencing to restrict access to the robot
	+ Install pressure-sensitive pads or area scanners to detect when someone enters a restricted zone
	+ Provide emergency stop buttons
	+ Use caution signs to warn others when performing maintenance on an active robot
	+ Lockout or tagout procedures ensure the robot cannot be restarted during service
	+ Train all workers on general robotic safety

**Programming Your Polishing Robot: Tips and Tricks**

* Start with simple movements and tasks before moving on to more complex polishing procedures
* Simulate the program before running it to check for potential issues
* Be extremely specific in your programming, including precise coordinates, angles, pressures, and dwell times
* Don't forget the importance of force control
* Expect mistakes and make adjustments as needed

**Maintaining Your Robotic Polishing System**

* Perform a quick inspection of the robot and work area before starting each shift (daily check)
* Check for signs of wear on the end-effector, such as excessive vibration or noise
* Monitor the robot's movement and Polish quality regularly
* Update the programming as needed to ensure optimal performance
