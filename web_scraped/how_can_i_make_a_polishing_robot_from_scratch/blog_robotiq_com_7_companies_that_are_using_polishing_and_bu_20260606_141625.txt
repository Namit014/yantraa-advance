Here is the extracted technical information:

* Robot polishing is a process that improves safety, quality, and consistency of polished products.
* Polishing and buffing are finishing tasks that improve the surface quality of a product.
* There are two main options for polishing in manufacturing:
	+ Manual polishing: uses a power tool and running it over the surface by hand, but can cause musculoskeletal problems and quality variability.
	+ Autonomous polishing machines: like CNC machines with polishing tools, but limited in flexibility and reprogramming.
* Robots are increasingly popular for polishing tasks due to their flexibility and large workspace, allowing for a wide range of polishing applications.
* 7 companies that are already using robot polishing:
	+ Acme: uses fixed, abrasive polishing wheel with robot holding workpiece and moving it into contact with spinning abrasive for polishing metal bars, wheels, and door handles.
	+ Paradigm Electronics: uses UR10 cobot with custom polishing end effector for polishing speaker lacquer layers.
	+ WMF: uses robot to hold product and move it onto spinning polishing abrasive for buffing pressure cooker lids.
	+ Walmart: uses Auto-C autonomous guided vehicle to polish store floors.
	+ Tanker Manufacturer: uses two industrial robots to polish tanker trailer surfaces with custom polishing end effectors.
	+ Aerospace Manufacturers: uses robot polishing for parts not painted, such as, for corrosion removal.
	+ Saint-Gobain: uses UR robot with FT300 force sensor for glass manufacturing, improving consistency and productivity of polishing task.
* Robots are used for various polishing applications, including:
	+ Metal finishing
	+ Deburring
	+ Grinding
	+ Buffing
	+ Polishing
* Cobot polishing is becoming increasingly popular, with companies seeing benefits in terms of quality, consistency, and productivity.
* Some companies use collaborative robots (cobots) for polishing tasks, such as Paradigm Electronics and Saint-Gobain.
