Here are the technical specifications, facts, numbers, and descriptions:

**Robotic Polishing:**

* Use of robot arms with force-controlled tooling to finish surfaces automatically
* Consistent force and speed control
* Able to maintain uniform pressure or RPM on complex contours
* Can increase abrasive life by 200-300%

**Types of Robotic Polishing:**

* Brushed Finish: Creates a uniform linear grain on metal surfaces using belts or wheels, ideal for architectural metals, consumer products, and any part needing a consistent directional finish
* Grained Finish: Produces a controlled, repeatable surface texture that hides imperfections and prepares stainless steel or aluminum for coating, fabrication, or cosmetic applications
* Stainless Steel Polishing (Ra Finish): Polishes stainless steel to a defined Ra value with precise control over force, speed, and media for repeatable surface performance
* Mirror Finish: Removes micro-scratches and buffs the surface to a high-gloss, mirror-like reflection with consistent clarity across the entire part

**Force Control:**

* Uniform pressure is critical for polishing
* Too much force can cause overheating and distortion; too little can leave an uneven sheen
* PushCorp's force compliance devices automatically adjust contact pressure as the robot moves over contours or varying surface angles

**Test Before You Deploy:**

* Every surface reacts differently to polishing compounds and abrasives
* Validation step reduces trial-and-error on the production floor and ensures the desired finish is achieved from day one

**Robotic Polishing Solution:**

**Robotic Force Compliance Devices:**
