**Polishing:**

* Steps:
	1. Clean the item
	2. Identify the right kind of polish agent
	3. Apply a little amount of polish and rub it into the surface
	4. Keep up light pressure uniformly until you attain your desired level of glossiness
	5. Use another separate dry cloth to buff away the remaining polish on the surface

**Polishing Agents:**

* Selecting the right polishing agent based on the material being polished (e.g. metal, wood, plastics)
* Examples:
	+ Metals: use a polish that goes hand in hand with the metal (brass, silver, aluminum)
	+ Wood: use a polish that not only shines but also protects the grain of the wood
	+ Plastics: use specialized polishes that avoid unwanted damage

**Polishing vs. Grinding:**

* Grinding is about shaping the object to your liking, using brute force
* Polishing is about giving the surface a shiny appearance, using gentler methods

**Essential Tools:**

* Rotary polishers
* Orbital polishers
* Polishing pads (e.g. foam, wool, microfiber)

**Technical Details:**

* Rotary polishers: designed for precision and control, perfect for scrubbing away deep scratches
* Orbital polishers: designed for gentler operation, perfect for beginners or not-so-damaging tasks
* Polishing pads: come in different materials and textures, each suitable for different stages of the polishing process
* Abrasive materials: remove layers from the surface one by one until it is smooth
