**Assessing Current Polishing Process for Automation**

* Factors to consider:
	+ Defined polishing process
	+ Availability of open space for a robot work cell
	+ Types of force and range of movement required for parts
	+ Precision required for polishing small or intricate parts
	+ Amount of handling workpieces
* Assessing these factors upfront helps determine the need for modification and investment to implement robotic polishing in a facility.

**Safety First: Risk Assessments and Safeguarding for Robotic Polishing**

* Conducting a risk assessment:
	+ Identifying dangers related to implementing a robot
	+ Considering hazards like body parts getting caught on an end-effector, heavy loads, sharp edges, and high voltages
* Implementing safeguards:
	+ Using barriers, pressure-sensitive pads, or area scanners to restrict access to the robot
	+ Providing emergency stop buttons and caution signs
	+ Training all workers on general robotic safety

**Programming Your Polishing Robot: Tips and Tricks**

* Tips:
	+ Start with simple movements and tasks before moving on to more complex polishing procedures
	+ Simulate the program before running it to detect potential issues
	+ Be extremely specific in your programming, including precise coordinates, angles, pressures, and dwell times
	+ Don't forget the importance of force control in polishing
	+ Expect mistakes and make adjustments as needed
* Programming considerations:
	+ Force sensor integration for precise levels of force and torque control
	+ Measuring and mapping out precise coordinates, angles, pressures, and dwell times

**Maintaining Your Robotic Polishing System**

* Daily checks:
	+ Performing a quick inspection of the robot and work area before starting each shift
* Robot-specific maintenance tasks:
	+ Performing regular maintenance and repairs as needed
	+ Replacing worn-out parts and components
	+ Cleaning and lubricating moving parts and components
