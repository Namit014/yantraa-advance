* 10 common sanding and polishing problems that robots can eliminate:
	1. Sanding syndrome
	2. Edge rounding
	3. Corner mistakes
	4. Flaw fixation
	5. Sanding bias
	6. Tilting edge
	7. Speeding up
	8. Inconsistent speed
	9. Pressing down
	10. Being human
* Description of sanding syndrome: "a psychological disorder caused by fussy attention to detail combined with brain-rotting boredom"
* Edge rounding: unintentional rounding of the edge of the workpiece, especially when sanding soft materials like wood or plastic
* Corner mistakes: unintentional rounding, cupping, or otherwise messing up the corners of the workpiece
* Flaw fixation: focusing on the flaws and ignoring the good things, leading to an uneven finish
* Sanding bias: paying more attention to one face of the workpiece because it is most visible
* Tilting edge: wearing down the edge of the abrasive and causing an inconsistent finish by tilting the sanding tool onto its edge
* Speeding up: speeding up to complete the task sooner, but producing an inconsistent surface finish
* Inconsistent speed: varying the speed of the sanding or polishing tool, leading to an uneven finish
* Pressing down: applying too much pressure onto the surface, leading to a bad surface finish or damage to the motor of the tool
