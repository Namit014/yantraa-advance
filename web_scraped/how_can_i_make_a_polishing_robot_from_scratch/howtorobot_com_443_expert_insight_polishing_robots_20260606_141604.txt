**Polishing Applications:**

* Excellent targets for automation
* Standardized task with repetitive qualities
* Suitable for manufacturers that produce parts in high volumes
* Can be programmed for the robot to handle minor, randomized defects
* Requires special programming and relevant sensing equipment
* May struggle with parts with scuffs or scratches or irregular shapes

**Robot Types:**

* Six-axis robots:
	+ Excellent for polishing applications with complex geometries
	+ Good balance of reach and payload capacity
	+ Can handle large parts with complex curves and sides
* SCARA robots:
	+ Cost-effective option for simple polishing applications
	+ Limited in reach and struggle with complex part surfaces
	+ Good for polishing small mirrors and flat metal panels
* Cylindrical robots:
	+ Less common choice
	+ Can struggle with complex part geometries
	+ Useful for simple polishing tasks on vertical parts (e.g., car doors)
