**Project Overview**
The Dtto Modular Robot is an open-source project inspired by the MTRAN III. The module is designed to be fully 3D printed and assembled using open-source hardware.

**Materials and Components Required**
To assemble one module, the following components are necessary:
*   **3D Printed Parts:** 22 parts total. ABS plastic is recommended for machinability.
*   **Electronics:** 1 Arduino Nano v3.0, 1 HC-05 Bluetooth Module, 1 NRF24L01+ Wireless Transceiver, and 1 WS2812 RGB LED.
*   **Actuators:** 2 TowerPro MG92B Servomotors and 3 TowerPro SG90 Servomotors.
*   **Power:** 2 Li-Po Batteries (3.7V, 600mAh, 25C).
*   **Mechanical/Hardware:** 24 disk magnets (4x3mm), 40 self-tapping screws (M1.7x4mm), 3 small rubber bands, and a mini switch.
*   **Electrical:** 4 resistors at 520 Ohms, 1 resistor at 330 Ohms, and 1 resistor at 1200 Ohms.

**Assembly Steps (Part 1)**

*   **Preparation:** All printed parts must be cleaned of supports and sanded. An illustrated guide and Bill of Materials are available via Hackaday.io and GitHub. 
*   **Fitting:** Begin by fitting two pieces of Part 004-1 with Part 011-1, ensuring they rotate freely around the center to minimize friction.
*   **Servo Modification (MG92B):** To achieve a full 180-degree rotation, the MG92B servos must be modified:
    1.  Remove the back cover to access the control circuit and potentiometer.
    2.  Unsolder the first and third pins of the potentiometer.
    3.  Solder a 520-ohm resistor to each of those pins, then solder the cable back to the resistor.
    4.  Secure the circuit to the side of the motor case using tape to prevent short circuits.
*   **Motor Attachment:** Secure the modified MG92B servos to the printed parts using screws (which may need to be trimmed to length).
*   **Centering the Servo Arms:** Fix the cross-shaped servo arms to Part 011-1 and Part 012-1. It is critical to ensure the servo is perfectly centered at the 90-degree position to allow the full range of rotation. This can be tested using Arduino "sweep" examples.
*   **Module Halves:** The module is divided into a "male" and "female" half. Cable management is required by routing the female motor cables through the central hole to the male part during assembly.
*   **Male Half Construction:** The assembly of the male half begins by fitting and screwing Part 003-1 to Part 004-1 using three screws.

**Software and Design**
The robot was designed using FreeCAD and utilizes open-source hardware. While this tutorial focuses on the mechanical assembly, it notes that the IR detection system for the module is currently disabled for this version.
