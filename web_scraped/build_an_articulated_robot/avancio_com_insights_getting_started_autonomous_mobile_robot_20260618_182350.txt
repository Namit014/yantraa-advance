# Getting Started: Autonomous Mobile Robot DIY Guide for Small Businesses

**Author:** Oleksii Reznichenko
**Date:** 11.09.2025

## Introduction

Small and Medium Enterprises (SMEs) across various sectors can benefit from Autonomous Mobile Robots (AMRs). In warehouses and distribution centers, AMRs automate goods movement and order fulfillment, optimizing logistics with a goods-to-person approach. Manufacturing plants enhance production workflow through efficient material transport. Small family farms and greenhouses utilize AMRs for moving crops, feed, and equipment.

One of the most famous early adoption stories is Kiva Robotics, founded in 2003 and acquired by Amazon in 2012 for nearly 800 million USD, pioneering the goods-to-person concept.

This series provides a step-by-step guide for building your own AMR using open-source designs and accessible manufacturing methods, featuring detailed drawings, 3D models, Bill of Materials (BOM), hardware architecture, navigation software, and user interface packages.

## Design and Manufacturing Approach

The design is optimized for manufacturability, requiring only basic technologies:

- Laser cutting
- Bending
- Turning works (optionally)
- 3D printing (optionally)
- Assembly

Using mostly 2 mm thick metal sheets, the design is robust yet simple to produce, allowing one person to build the robot in one day if all parts are already produced.

The top of the robot features a mounting place for installing and connecting various external modules, such as:

- Conveyor
- Tote pulling hook
- Elevator
- Tilting mechanism
- Other custom modules

A mounting platform and holes for wire routing and connector installation are provided in the upper part of the robot.

**Note on Sheet Metal Parts:** Parts are designed for the following coefficients and radii: K=0.47: sheet 0.8 mm – R=1.2; sheet 1.2 mm – R=1.5; sheet 2.0 mm – R=2.4.

After bending, parts can be painted. After painting, riveting nuts are installed. It is allowed to install riveting nuts before painting, provided that the threaded part of the nuts is protected from coating. Screws installed without lock washers should be fixed with a thread lock (e.g., Loxeal 55-03).
