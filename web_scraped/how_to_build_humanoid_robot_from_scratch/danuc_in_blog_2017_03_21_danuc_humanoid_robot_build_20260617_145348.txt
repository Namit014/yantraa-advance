The Danuc Humanoid Robot is a 17 degrees of freedom (DOF) humanoid robot developed by Danuc Robotics And Automation. Key technical specifications and requirements include:

- **Construction Materials**:  
  - 2mm thickness high-grade aluminum brackets (stamped using a 25-ton electric power press and bent with a bending die).  
  - 3mm thick acrylic torso (laser-cut).  
  - 3D-printed standard servo brackets.  

- **Motors**:  
  - 17 standard-size double ball bearing metal gear servo motors.  
  - Torque: 15 kg-cm per motor.  

- **Controller**:  
  - Arduino Mega 2560 with a servo controller shield and onboard DC-DC converter.  
  - Operated via a 30-cell lithium polymer (LiPo) battery.  

- **Sensors**:  
  - 9 DOF Inertial Measurement Unit (IMU) with accelerometer, gyroscope, and magnetometer.  
  - Ultrasonic range finder mounted on tilt-and-pan mechanism.  

- **Software**:  
  - Inverse Kinematics (IK)-based fully tested code.  

- **Requirements for Build**:  
  1. Fully assembled 2mm thickness robot torso with 50mm tall studs (1 set).  
  2. 30-cell LiPo battery.  
  3. Complete servo erector set for two legs and hands.  
  4. Arduino Mega 2560 controller.  
  5. Servo controller shield (sandwiched on Arduino Mega 2560).  
  6. Battery charger.  
  7. Ultrasonic range finder with tilt-and-pan mechanism.  
  8. IK-based software.  
  9. 17 standard-size 15 kg-cm torque servo motors.  
  10. 3mm ID 8mm OD flanged bearings on each motor pivot.  
  11. 9 DOF IMU with accelerometer, gyroscope, and magnetometer.  

The robot was developed over approximately one year (2012–2013), with iterative design testing to address center-of-gravity imbalance issues, resulting in a finalized configuration optimized for stability and performance.
