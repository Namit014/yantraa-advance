Industrial robotic arms are programmable mechanical manipulators designed for precision in manufacturing and logistics. They consist of a multi-axis mechanical arm, actuators, sensors, an end-effector, and a controller. Key components of industrial robotic arms include:
*   **Mechanical arm:** Features multiple joints (axes) for movement.
*   **Actuators:** Power the arm's movement.
*   **Sensors:** Provide positioning feedback.
*   **End-effector:** A specialized tool such as a gripper or welding torch.
*   **Controller:** Executes programmed instructions.

**Types of Industrial Robotic Arms:**

*   **Articulated Robots:**
    *   Feature 4-7 rotary joints, resembling a human arm.
    *   Offer exceptional flexibility and reach in a spherical workspace.
    *   Ideal for complex movements, accessing confined spaces, and various angles of approach.
    *   Common applications: Arc welding, spot welding, material handling, painting, coating, assembly, machine tending.
    *   Payload capacities range from less than 5kg to over 2,000kg.
*   **SCARA Robots (Selective Compliance Assembly Robot Arm):**
    *   Optimized for high-speed, high-precision horizontal plane operations.
    *   Typically have four axes: two parallel rotary joints for X-Y plane movement, one prismatic joint for Z-axis movement, and a rotary joint for tool rotation.
    *   Provide compliance in the X-Y plane and rigidity in the Z-axis, excelling at vertical assembly and pick-and-place.
    *   Features a cylindrical work envelope.
    *   Cycle times can be 50-75% faster than articulated robots for lateral movements.
    *   Effective for insertion tasks requiring controlled downward force.
