The SCARA robot described has 4 degrees of freedom. It is driven by four NEMA 17 stepper motors, each paired with an A4988 driver on a CNC shield controlled by an Arduino UNO. The end‑effector gripper is actuated by an MG996R servo motor.

**Mechanical reductions**  
- Joint 1: 20 : 1 reduction, achieved in two stages with custom GT2 pulleys.  
- Joint 2: 16 : 1 reduction, also achieved in two stages with custom GT2 pulleys.  
- Joint 3: 4 : 1 reduction, achieved with a single‑stage reduction.  

**Bearings**  
- Thrust ball bearing: 1 × 40 × 60 × 13 mm.  
- Thrust ball bearing: 2 × 35 × 52 × 12 mm.  
- Radial ball bearing: 5 × 8 × 22 × 7 mm.  
- Additional 608 ball bearings (2) used for the middle pulley.

**Linear motion components**  
- Four smooth rods: 10 mm diameter, 400 mm length.  
- Linear ball bearings: 10 mm (four units).  
- Lead screw for Z‑axis: 8 mm diameter, 400 mm nominal length; to fit the configuration the screw must be 20 mm shorter (≈380 mm) or the Z motor can be raised by 20 mm using spacer nuts.  

**Belts and pulleys**  
- GT2 closed‑loop belts: 200 mm and 300 mm lengths.  
- GT2 pulleys are 3D printable; tooth count can be varied parametrically to adjust size.

**Fasteners (examples)**  
- Radial ball bearing for joint 1: 35 mm inner, 47 mm outer diameter.  
- Thrust bearing for joint 1: 40 mm inner, 60 mm outer diameter.  
- Joint coupling secured with four M4 × 55 mm bolts and self‑locking nuts.  
- Middle pulley secured with a 45 mm M8 bolt, washer, and self‑locking nut (using two 608 bearings).  

**Other hardware**  
- Various M3, M4, and M5 bolts and nuts as required for assembly.  

**3D‑printed parts**  
- Designed for printing on printers with smaller beds (e.g., Creality CR‑10, Ender 3).  
- Printed primarily in PLA+ (blue) for most parts; PLA used for pulleys and gripper.  
- Print time ≈120 hours at 60 mm/s; base alone ≈32 hours.  
- Horizontal expansion of –0.1 mm applied in slicer for better fit.  

**Software/Control**  
- GUI created in Processing environment providing forward and inverse kinematics control via sliders.  
- Allows simultaneous display of joint angles and end‑effector X, Y, Z coordinates.  
- Features “Save” and “Run” buttons for storing and looping movements, with adjustable speed and acceleration.
