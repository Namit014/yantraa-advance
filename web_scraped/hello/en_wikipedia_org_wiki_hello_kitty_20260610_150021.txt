**Creation and Design**

* Hello Kitty was created in 1974 by Yuko Shimizu.
* Designed by Yuko Yamaguchi, who has been lead designer since 1980.
* Inspired by fashion, movies, and TV.
* New series and designs released regularly, following current trends.

**History and Reception**

* First appeared on a vinyl coin purse in 1975 in Japan.
* First appeared in the United States in 1976.
* Sold well immediately after launch, increasing Sanrio's sales sevenfold.
* Popularity grew with the emergence of kawaii culture.
* Named children's ambassador to the United States in 1983, Japan in 1994, and Special Friend of Children in 2004.
* Original target market was children and pre-teens, later broadened to include teenagers and adults.
* Marked a global phenomenon in 2010, worth $5 billion a year.

**Product Line**

* Over 50,000 different Hello Kitty branded products as of 2008.
* Product line includes school supplies, clothing, accessories, toys, and other items.
* Annual sales: $5 billion (2010), $8 billion (2013)

**Marketing and Promotions**

* Initial marketing focused on pre-teenage girls, later broadened to include teenagers and adults.
* Use of dark designs for Hello Kitty products, following trends in Japan, began in 2007.

**Business and Finance**

* Sanrio theme parks: Harmonyland in Hiji, Ōita, Japan, Sanrio Puroland in Tama New Town, Tokyo, Japan, and Hello Kitty Shanghai Times in Shanghai, China.
* Annual revenue growth: 7-fold increase in 1975.

**Muktoseki and Speculation**

* Hello Kitty has been referred to as an early example of muktoseki, a Japanese term meaning "stateless" or "nationless".
* Speculation about Hello Kitty's origins, including a possible connection to Maneki Neko, a traditional Japanese cat figurine.
