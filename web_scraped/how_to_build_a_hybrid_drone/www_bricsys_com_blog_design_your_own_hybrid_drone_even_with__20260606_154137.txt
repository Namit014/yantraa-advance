**Hybrid Drone Design**

* A combination of multicopters and fixed-wing planes
* Multicopters: excellent flight flexibility and stable hovering capability, but aerodynamically unstable and require an onboard computer to stabilize them
* Fixed-wing planes: more stable and energy-efficient during level flight
* Hybrid drones combine these two designs to maximize efficiency

**Control System**

* Typically designed in three separate modes: "copter", "plane", and "transitional" (where both sets of blades are in motion)
* The new system uses a neural network that transitions between the three stages automatically
* The system uses machine learning, which means that with every simulation of every design, the program gets better
* The team hopes to use calculations from the simulation of one design to assist with the calculations of the next

**Simulation and Testing**

* The team used a neural network to simulate flight and optimize the control system of a hybrid drone
* The user creates their own designs by mixing, matching, and manipulating parameters from a library of components
* The team designed a library of subcomponents, such as wings, fuselages, propeller mounts, etc.
* The shape and size of these parts are controlled by parametrics
* Constraints dictate how the parts can be assembled
* Five hybrid models were used in the training, all chosen for their very different shape and size
* The team created real-life models of their drones and flew them using the control system that the simulation generated
* The drones were fabricated using 3D printing, laser cutting, and manual assembly

**Materials and Construction**

* Internal chassis made from carbon fiber tubing with fiberglass-reinforced, 3D printed, nylon connectors
* Wings constructed from laser cut foam or balsa wood and heat-shrunk plastic film
* Propellers: brushless electric motors

**Control System Hardware**

* The flight controller was built on a modified version of ArduCopter, an open-source, Pixhawk flight computer hardware
* Propellers: brushless electric motors

**Future Development**

* The team would like to develop the principles further to add in calculations for moving parts such as rudders
* They would also like to generate an algorithm that could automatically suggest optimization of component parts during the design process
