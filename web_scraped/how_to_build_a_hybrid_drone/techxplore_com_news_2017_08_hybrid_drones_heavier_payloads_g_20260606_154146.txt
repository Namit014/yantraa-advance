**Drone Specifications:**

* Hybrid gas-to-electric drones from Top Flight Technologies offer:
	+ Order-of-magnitude increase in range
	+ Order-of-magnitude increase in payload size and power
* Can fly for:
	+ Over 2.5 hours
	+ Up to 100 miles
	+ Carrying up to 20 pounds
* Engine weighs about 17 pounds and can generate up to 10 kilowatts of power
* Onboard batteries never need recharging; users just need to refill the gas tank and fly again

**Technical Challenges:**

* Excessive vibration and heat in microscale hybrid electric-gas engines
* Solved using heat transfer and control techniques, such as:
	+ Strategically incorporating small fans, cooling fins, and rubber vibration dampeners

**Digital Gearbox:**

* Behaves like traditional mechanical transmission system but uses electricity to control everything
* Gasoline runs to a small generator, creating electric power, which the digital gearbox controls and sends in pulses to the electric motors and electronics

**Implications:**

* Increased range and payload capacity for drones and microsatellites
* Enhanced imaging and sensing resolutions, and other capabilities, such as communications
* Potential applications for:
	+ Inspecting infrastructure in remote areas
	+ Pre- and post-disaster recovery efforts
	+ Delivery drones

**Historical Milestones:**

* Top Flight Technologies founded in 2014 by Long Phan and other MIT engineers
* Raised over $2 million in early venture funding by 2015
