**Type of Industrial Robotic Arm:**

* SCARA (Selective Compliance Assembly Robot Arm) is a type of industrial robot.

**Technical Specifications:**

* Selective compliance: compliant in the X-Y direction but rigid in the Z direction.
* Articulated two-link arm layout, similar to human arms.

**Advantages:**

* Slightly compliant in the X-Y direction, allowing for insertion of round pins in round holes without binding.
* Rigid in the Z direction, allowing for precise movement and control.
* Can extend into confined areas and retract or "fold up" out of the way.

**Comparison to Cartesian Robot Systems:**

* SCARAs are generally faster than comparable Cartesian robot systems.
* SCARAs can be more expensive than comparable Cartesian systems.
* Controlling software requires inverse kinematics for linear interpolated moves.

**History:**

* 1977: A. d'Auria presents the SIGMA robot for assembly at the 7th International Symposium on Robotics in Tokyo.
* 1978: The first SCARA prototype is built.
* 1980: The second prototype is built.
* 1981: SCARA robots are developed by the industry, resulting from research and development by the University of Yamanashi and 13 Japanese companies.

**Awards and Recognition:**

* 2006: The SCARA robot is inducted into the Robot Hall of Fame.
