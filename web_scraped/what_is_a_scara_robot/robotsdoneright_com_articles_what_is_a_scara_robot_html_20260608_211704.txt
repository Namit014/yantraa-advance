**What is a SCARA Robot?**

* A type of industrial robot
* Originally developed in 1978 for assembly applications
* Definition: Selective Compliance Articulated Robot Arm (formerly Selective Compliance Assembly Robot Arm)

**Specifications**

* Small industrial robots, typically pedestal or floor mounted, with some models wall-mounted (e.g., FANUC's SR series)
* Payload capacity: 0.5 kg to 20 kg
* Reach: generally 1,000 mm or less
* Number of axes: typically 4, with 3-axis models available (e.g., FANUC SR-3ia)
* Axes configuration: dynamic and rigid movements (X and Y axes flexible, Z axis fixed)

**Benefits**

* Fastest type of robot due to fewer joints to control and move
* Best price-to-performance ratio due to fewer components and materials used in their construction
* Affordable alternative to other robot types, including used options (e.g., buying a used FANUC SR-6ia)
* Suitable for assembly processes, material handling, and other industrial applications (e.g., 3D printing, inspection, palletizing, dispensing)

**Applications**

* Assembly automation (e.g., inserting bolts, high-speed assemblies, as used in FANUC SR-12ia)
* Material handling (e.g., pick and place, packaging)
* Inspection and dispensing
* 3D printing and palletizing
