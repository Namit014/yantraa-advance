**SCARA Robots:**

* Definition: Selective Compliance Assembly/Articulated Robot Arm (SCARA)
* Characteristic: Big swivel joint and ball screw
* Unique feature: Speed, compactness, and precision
* Industries that use SCARA robots: Electronics, Telecommunications, Food and beverage, Retail manufacturing, Automotive, Pharmaceuticals

**Applications:**

* Assembly
* Disassembly
* Picking and placing
* Material handling
* PCB soldering
* Dispensing

**Strengths:**

* Speed
* Precision
* Compactness

**Limitations:**

* Small range of motion
* Small payload capacity
* Selective compliance (can be a limitation for applications requiring mechanical flexibility)

**When to use a SCARA:**

* If the application requires small, high-speed, and high-precision operations
* If the application is in one of the industries listed above
* If the robot is designed for small component assembly and handling

**Cost:**

* Lower upfront cost compared to other robot types due to fewer components and smaller size
* Requires integration costs, including physical installation, programming, setup, and safety mitigation
* Requires ongoing maintenance schedules, including greasing, mechanical inspections, and performance tests

**When to use a SCARA:**

* For entry-level robot users to begin their automation journey
* For projects with a specific business case and application fit
