**Definition**: SCARA stands for Selective Compliance Assembly Robot Arm, a type of industrial robotic arm known for its ability to move freely and maintain stiffness in three axes while being compliant in the final axis.

**History**:

* 1977: Professor Hiroshi Makino of Yamanashi University witnessed the SIGMA assembly robot invention at the International Symposium on Industrial Robots in Tokyo, Japan.
* 1978: Makino formed the SCARA Robot Consortium, consisting of 13 Japanese companies, to improve assembly robots through dedicated research.
* 1981: The first commercial SCARA robot was released, hailed as a groundbreaking robotic design with a favorable price-performance ratio.

**Key Features**:

* Typically features 4 axes, with 2 parallel arms that can move in a single plane.
* Final axis is at right angles to the others and is compliant.
* Easy to program due to simpler inverse kinematics compared to 6-degree-of-freedom industrial robotic arms.
* Fixed position of joints makes them very predictable, with positions in the robot's workspace approachable from a single orientation.

**Advantages**:

* Fast cycle times, impressive positioning accuracy, and high repeatability compared to other robot types.
* Well-suited for pick and place operations that require precision, speed, and smooth motion.
* Popular for applications in electronics assembly, food manufacturing, and other industries.

**Disadvantages**:

* Limited payload capacity, typically ranging from 30-50 kg.
* Restricted workspace, which may limit the size of operations and flexibility in approaching tasks.
