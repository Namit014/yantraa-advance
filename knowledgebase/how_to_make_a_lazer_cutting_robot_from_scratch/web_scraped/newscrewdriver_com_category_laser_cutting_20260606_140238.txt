**Laser Cutting and 3D Printing**

* MeArm robot arm built out of commodity micro servos and laser-cut acrylic
* Laser-cut DXF file available for open-sourced design
* Limitations: multiple CAD software unable to load DXF file

**Circuit Board and 3D Printing**

* Melding circuit components into 3D-printed plastic
* Using soldering iron to heat up wires and melt into plastic
* Components melted into plastic more secure than traditional placement
* Electrical tape used to guard against short-circuiting

**LED Test Fixtures**

* Square, circular, and triangular LED cavities tested
* Teardrop shape cavity resulted in significant light loss
* Minimizing cavity sizes and conforming curve cavities achieved best results
* Small curve in front of LED point broke up central beam and sent light to edges
* Frosted edge reduced reflections and enhanced overall illumination

**Acrylic Edge Illumination**

* Positioning LED at edge of acrylic sheet
* Goal: direct light to illuminate all edges of acrylic sheet
* 3D-printed test fixture with 75mm x 75mm test piece, 10mm wide border, and LED
* Observation area at outside edge and bottom of fixture
* Aluminum foil lining the bottom of the test fixture to reflect light back into acrylic

**Limitations and Future Work**

* Additional experimentation and testing needed to determine best methods for edge illumination and reducing light loss
* Future work to apply learned concepts to future acrylic projects
