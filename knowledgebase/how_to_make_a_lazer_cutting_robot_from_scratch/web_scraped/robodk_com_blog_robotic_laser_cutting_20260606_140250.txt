**Conventional Laser Cutting Machines**

* Expensive: $2,000 to $200,000 or more
* Single-purpose: Can only cut or etch on a single plane
* Huge: Can take up a whole section of a machine shop
* Require supervision: Need a skilled operator, adding to cost
* Limitations: Require multiple machines for different tasks, adding to budget and space required

**Robot Laser Cutting Machines**

* More flexible: Can cut or etch in any orientation within its workspace
* Can run autonomously: Can be programmed to run without human intervention
* Take up less floor space: Can be used in smaller spaces, as only one machine is needed
* Can perform complex tasks: Can be programmed to perform complex tasks, such as depalletizing and material handling
* Programming: Requires a good programming system to quickly program complex paths

**Comparison of Conventional and Robot Laser Cutting Machines**

* Conventional machines: Limiting, expensive, and require multiple machines for different tasks
* Robot machines: Flexible, affordable, and can perform multiple tasks with one machine
