Here are the relevant technical specifications, facts, numbers, and descriptions extracted from the text:

**Machine Details**

* Fixed gantry moving table design
* Baseplate: 10mm alloy, surface-ground
* Gantry: 10mm anodized T6061 T651 plate, L-shaped
* Side supports: 16mm alloy, triangular pieces, cut on bandsaw and filed
* Rear gantry strengthening: 100x50 alloy box section, glued and screwed
* Linear rails: anodized alloy, polymer bearings
* Table plate: 10mm acrylic, resting on 2 smaller pieces of 10mm acrylic
* Leadscrews: rolled ball screws with metric 8mm lead, 100 steps = 1 mm
* Stepper motor bearings: rated for 8kg axial load
* Couplings: solid, made from brass on a 7x20 lathe
* Stepper motors: 300 oz-in unipolar, NEMA23 long

**Travel and Dimensions**

* Z travel: 65 mm
* X travel: 370 mm
* Y travel: 265 mm
* Total gantry height: 40 mm

**Spindle and Clamp**

* Spindle: 650 watt Ozito router, quiet and low vibration
* Spindle clamp: made from 16mm alloy plate, 42mm hole
* Router sits close to the rear plate for better rigidity and travels

**Motor and Driver Details**

* Motors: unipolar, 2A, 6v
* Microstepping: 16th microstepping
* Drivers: SLAmStepper microstepping kits, compact, mounted on the back of the gantry
* Heatsink: a 25x6 mm bar is screwed to the gantry box, acting as a simple heatsink

**Wiring and Power**

* DC supply: external, in its own box on the floor
* Motor wire lengths: varying, with a few inches of flex cable for the Z axis motor
* CAT5 cable: used for the longest run, the front table Y motor

**PIC Brain and Indexer**

* PIC chip: runs at 10 MIPS, receives serial commands from the remote PC, controls stepper drivers
* Bi-directional comms: with the PC, at 57600 baud, over a 20 metre cable
* Indexer: controls stepper drivers, handles bi-directional comms, turns SLAmSteppers down to low power in rest pauses, and sends step and direction signals.
