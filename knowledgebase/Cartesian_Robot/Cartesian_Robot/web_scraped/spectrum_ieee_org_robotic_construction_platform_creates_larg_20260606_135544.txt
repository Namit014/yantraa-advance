* Technical Specifications:
	+ Digital Construction Platform (DCP) is an automated construction system capable of customized on-site fabrication of architectural-scale structures.
	+ The DCP uses additive construction techniques to build large structures safely, quickly, and even (in some cases) renewably.
	+ The robot arm has a maximum printable volume of 2,786 cubic meters.
	+ The system cost is US $244,500.
* Facts:
	+ The DCP has a battery-powered design with an option for more solar panels to be attached.
	+ The robot has a top speed of 0.5 meters per second.
	+ The DCP can potentially run forever, or as long as you have sun.
* Numbers:
	+ 14.6-meter-wide, 3.7-meter-tall hemispherical open dome
	+ 1.728 cubic meters per hour printing rate
	+ 2,786 cubic meters maximum printable volume
* Descriptions:
	+ The DCP uses a sprayer at the end of the small arm that combines two chemicals into a liquid polyurethane foam that rapidly expands and hardens.
	+ The construction technique involves creating a concrete formwork: Two foam walls, one nested inside the other, with a space in the middle that can be poured with concrete or backfilled with dirt or other materials.
	+ The foam dries quickly, allowing for the creation of a dome or flat roof.
	+ Rebar and chains can be autonomously embedded or welded into rigid shapes for additional support.
